Q2. (10 Marks | 150 words)

Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Smt. Priya Sharma, an accomplished IAS officer, is serving as the District Magistrate in Rudrapur, a district witnessing rapid industrialization and significant land acquisition for mega-projects. Her reputation for integrity and efficiency precedes her, and she has been instrumental in ensuring fair compensation processes for displaced farmers.

Recently, her office received a complaint regarding a major real estate project owned by the son of a powerful state minister. The project, a high-rise residential complex, allegedly violates several environmental clearances and zoning regulations, posing significant ecological risks to a nearby wetland and displacing a small tribal community. The minister has personally called Smt. Sharma, strongly 'advising' her to expedite the clearances and overlook the procedural lapses, implying severe consequences for her career if she doesn't comply. Her Chief Secretary has also subtly hinted at the political sensitivity of the matter.

The tribal community is protesting, while environmental activists are threatening legal action. Smt. Sharma’s decision will directly impact the rule of law, environmental sustainability, the rights of the vulnerable, and the public's trust in governance, besides her own professional standing.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Smt. Priya Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
