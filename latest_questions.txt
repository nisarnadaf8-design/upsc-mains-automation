Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Rajesh Kumar, a dynamic young IAS officer of the 2020 batch, is currently serving as the District Magistrate of Rudrapur District, a bustling industrial hub known for its textile manufacturing units. Rajesh has been particularly proactive in promoting child welfare and ensuring the enforcement of labour laws since assuming office six months ago. His dedication has earned him both public appreciation and some resentment from industrialists who prefer lax oversight.

During an unannounced inspection of a major textile factory, ostensibly to check environmental compliance, Rajesh's team discovered a hidden section employing a significant number of children, some as young as eight, in hazardous conditions. It quickly came to light that the factory is owned by Mr. Surendra Singh, a powerful state cabinet minister and a close aide to the Chief Minister, who also wields considerable influence over local administration. Shortly after the discovery, Rajesh received an indirect call from his Divisional Commissioner suggesting that he handle the matter with extreme discretion due to "political sensitivities" and implying that any drastic action could have "unforeseen career repercussions." Simultaneously, local media has caught wind of the child labour allegations, and a prominent national child rights NGO has issued a public statement demanding immediate action and threatening a national campaign if the district administration fails to act decisively.

Rajesh is fully aware that taking immediate, firm action against such a powerful political figure could not only jeopardize his promising career but also potentially lead to a political witch-hunt. Conversely, any attempt to cover up or dilute the findings would be a grave betrayal of his ethical oath, compromise the integrity of his office, and leave vulnerable children exploited. The children, their desperate families, the factory owner, the minister, the local administration, the NGO, and the public are all stakeholders whose interests and futures hang on Rajesh's decision. Both action and inaction carry severe personal and professional consequences, and the ethical choice is mired in practical complexities.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Rajesh Kumar? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
