Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Mr. Alok Kumar, a dedicated District Environment Officer in the State Pollution Control Board, has recently been posted to an industrializing district in a coastal state. His mandate includes monitoring and enforcing environmental regulations. A major cement plant, Bharati Cements Ltd., is a significant employer and revenue generator for the region and has a history of minor environmental complaints.

During a routine inspection, Mr. Kumar's team discovers that Bharati Cements is discharging a significant volume of untreated industrial waste directly into a local river, far exceeding permissible limits. This pollution is severely impacting the livelihoods of local fishing communities and contaminating agricultural lands downstream. The plant manager, Mr. Rajesh Sharma, offers a substantial "donation" to a local charity, implicitly expecting Mr. Kumar to overlook the violations, and hints at the company's strong political connections. The local MLA also calls Mr. Kumar, urging a "pragmatic approach" to avoid disrupting employment and investment. Furthermore, Mr. Brijesh Singh, Mr. Kumar's Senior Environment Officer, advises caution, citing the company's influence and the state government's overarching push for industrial growth.

Mr. Kumar's decision will directly affect the health and livelihoods of the local communities, the credibility of the State Pollution Control Board, the economic stability of Bharati Cements and its employees, and his own career and ethical standing. Both immediate action and inaction carry significant and complex consequences.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Mr. Alok Kumar? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
