Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Ms. Anjali Sharma, a newly appointed Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM) in the industrially burgeoning district of Rudrapur, Uttarakhand, was keen on ensuring fair labor practices. Her proactive approach involved regular field visits to factories and establishments in her jurisdiction, aiming to foster both economic growth and social welfare.

During an unannounced inspection of a large garment manufacturing unit, 'Garuda Textiles', Ms. Sharma's team uncovered irrefutable evidence of several children, some as young as nine, working in deplorable conditions. To her dismay, she soon learned that 'Garuda Textiles' was widely known to be owned by Mr. Raghav Puri, a powerful state cabinet minister. Her immediate superior, the District Collector, called her, advising her to "exercise extreme caution" and suggesting the matter be "handled internally and sensitively" to avoid political complications, implicitly hinting at adverse career repercussions. Simultaneously, a local child rights NGO, having received prior intelligence, began pressing Ms. Sharma for swift and decisive action.

The primary stakeholders include the exploited children, their families, the factory management, Minister Puri, Ms. Sharma, the district administration, and the child rights NGO. Both immediate action and delayed response carry significant consequences, ranging from ensuring justice and upholding the law to risking political backlash and compromising administrative integrity.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Ms. Anjali Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
