GS-1 SOCIETY: Tribal Communities: Issues, Rights and Constitutional Safeguards
========================================

Q1. (10 Marks | 150 words)
Examine the persistent challenges in effectively implementing the spirit of the Fifth and Sixth Schedule provisions amidst evolving developmental priorities and resource demands. Discuss how these constitutional safeguards can be strengthened to better protect tribal autonomy and land rights in the contemporary context.

Q2. (10 Marks | 150 words)
What do you understand by the concept of 'cultural integration' for tribal communities, as opposed to forced assimilation? Critically examine how large-scale infrastructure projects and market forces challenge the distinct cultural identity and traditional livelihoods of Particularly Vulnerable Tribal Groups (PVTGs) in India.

Q3. (15 Marks | 250 words)
Critically analyse the socio-economic and legal implications of involuntary displacement for tribal communities in the pursuit of national development projects. Discuss, with suitable examples, how inadequate implementation of the Forest Rights Act 2006 exacerbates their vulnerability and often leads to an intergenerational loss of traditional knowledge and resources.

GS-4 ETHICS: Ethics in Public and Private Relationships
========================================

Q4. (10 Marks | 150 words)
Explain the significance of maintaining 'ethical distance' in public administration, particularly when personal affiliations and private interests intersect with official decision-making. How does a civil servant's commitment to personal integrity influence the perception of impartiality and trustworthiness within the bureaucracy?

Q5. (10 Marks | 150 words)
What are the ethical challenges faced by a civil servant when dealing with requests from close family members or friends that could potentially compromise the principles of meritocracy and fairness in administrative processes? How should a civil servant navigate such situations to uphold public trust without alienating personal relationships?

Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Ms. Anjali Sharma, a Divisional Forest Officer in Mandla District, Madhya Pradesh, recently unearthed a large-scale financial fraud involving illegal timber trade and embezzlement of funds meant for tribal welfare schemes in her division, meticulously documenting compelling evidence.

Upon reporting this meticulously documented complaint to her immediate superior, she found her allegations dismissed without investigation. Subsequently, she was issued a punitive transfer to an obscure, non-field posting and a departmental inquiry was initiated against her on fabricated charges of insubordination. Simultaneously, a powerful local political leader, allegedly deeply involved in the fraud, has commenced a virulent smear campaign against her through local media. Her family, gravely concerned about her safety and career prospects, is strongly urging her to retract the complaint and comply with the directives to protect her future.

The tribal communities, for whom the welfare funds were intended, remain unaware of the underlying corruption, while the integrity of the Forest Department and public trust are at stake. Ms. Sharma's decision carries serious consequences, impacting not only her personal life and career but also justice for the defrauded communities and the moral standing of public service.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Ms. Anjali Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
