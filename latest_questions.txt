Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Rajesh Kumar, a dedicated and upright District Collector in the remote tribal-dominated Banswara district of Rajasthan, is overseeing the crucial land acquisition process for the ambitious "Solar Shakti Grid" project. This project promises to bring significant economic development and renewable energy infrastructure to a region historically marked by poverty and underdevelopment.

As the final list of land parcels for acquisition is being prepared, Rajesh discovers that a significant plot, strategically vital for the project's main substation, belongs to his maternal uncle, who has a history of litigious behaviour. His uncle, aware of Rajesh's position, has explicitly conveyed through family channels that he expects a substantially higher compensation than market rates, threatening to stall the project through legal means if his demands are not met. Rajesh's superior is pressing for expedited land acquisition to meet central government deadlines, while local politicians are urging swift resolution to avoid public discontent among other farmers.

The decision will directly impact thousands of local farmers awaiting fair compensation, the timely completion of a vital public infrastructure project, the state exchequer, and critically, Rajesh's reputation for impartiality and the public's trust in the administration. Both yielding to family pressure and strictly adhering to rules have serious consequences.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case.
Ethical issues include conflict of interest, nepotism/favouritism, integrity vs. family loyalty, misuse of public office for private gain, and maintaining impartiality. These threaten public trust, administrative fairness, and project efficiency, undermining the principles of good governance.

(b) What are the options available to Rajesh Kumar? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits.
1. Recuse himself: Merits - Upholds integrity, avoids conflict. Demerits - May delay project, transfer burden.
2. Direct subordinates to handle impartially: Merits - Maintains control, ensures fair process. Demerits - Perceived influence risk remains.
3. Strictly enforce rules on compensation: Merits - Upholds law, prevents favouritism. Demerits - Uncle may still cause legal delays, family strife.
4. Attempt mediation with uncle for fair resolution: Merits - Could resolve amicably. Demerits - May be seen as inappropriate personal intervention.

(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved?
I would recommend Rajesh Kumar to formally declare the conflict of interest to his superior and recuse himself from direct involvement in his uncle's land acquisition. He should request that the matter be handled by another competent officer or committee to ensure objective evaluation and adherence to rules. Simultaneously, he must unequivocally communicate to his family, especially his uncle, that no special favour can be granted, and the process will be purely legally compliant.

To handle pressures, he should document all communications regarding the conflict and recusal. He would professionally explain to his superior the necessity of recusal to maintain institutional integrity and avoid future legal challenges to the project. For political leaders and the public, he would emphasize transparency, fairness, and the equal application of law to all landowners, ensuring the project's long-term success and public trust.
