Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Smita Sharma, a newly appointed District Environmental Officer in the State Pollution Control Board, has been posted to Rajnagar, a rapidly industrializing district known for its chemical and textile manufacturing units. Smita is known for her integrity and dedication to environmental protection. Her primary responsibility involves enforcing environmental regulations and ensuring industries comply with pollution control norms.

During a routine inspection, her team discovers that "Green Harvest Ltd.," a major food processing company and a significant employer in the district, is regularly discharging untreated chemical waste into the local river, affecting the drinking water supply of several downstream villages. Her immediate superior, Mr. Rajesh Kumar, advises her to exercise "discretion" given the company's contribution to the local economy and its strong political connections. A local political leader also contacts her, emphasizing the potential job losses and social unrest if strict action is taken.

Smita is aware that the untreated discharge poses serious health risks to the villagers and harms the river ecosystem, but also recognizes the potential economic fallout if the factory is shut down or heavily penalized. She feels a strong duty to uphold the law and protect public health, yet the pressures to compromise are immense and multi-directional.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Smita Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
