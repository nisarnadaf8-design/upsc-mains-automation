Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Ms. Anjali Sharma, District Magistrate of the remote tribal district of Garhwalpur, is overseeing the acquisition of land for a crucial segment of the proposed National High-Speed Rail Corridor. This project is touted as a game-changer for regional connectivity and economic development, with significant national implications. The identified route passes through several villages predominantly inhabited by the indigenous Bhil community, recognized for their unique cultural heritage and traditional forest-dwelling lifestyle.

While the project promises economic benefits, the Bhil community leaders have staunchly opposed it, citing displacement, loss of ancestral land, disruption of their spiritual sites, and irreversible damage to their forest-based livelihoods. They argue that the compensation package offered is inadequate and fails to account for their cultural and ecological losses. Ms. Sharma's superior, the Divisional Commissioner, is pressuring her to expedite land acquisition, emphasizing the project's strategic national importance and strict deadlines, while a powerful local MLA is mobilizing the community, accusing the administration of bulldozing tribal rights.

The decision Ms. Sharma takes will directly impact the lives and cultural continuity of thousands of Bhil tribals, the economic trajectory of the entire region, and the national prestige associated with the project's timely completion. Failure to act decisively could stall a vital national project, while forceful acquisition risks serious social unrest, legal challenges, and a deep erosion of trust between the state and its most vulnerable citizens.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Ms. Anjali Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
