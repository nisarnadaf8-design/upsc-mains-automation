Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Rohan Sharma, a relatively new but respected Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM) in the Revenue Department, is known for his strict adherence to rules and proactive approach to public welfare in Mandar District, a semi-urban area. During a routine inspection of industrial units in his sub-division, following a tip-off about unregistered units, he discovers a large textile factory.

The factory, 'Grand Textiles', is found to employ a significant number of underage children in hazardous conditions. During the raid, Rohan's team collects irrefutable evidence. Immediately after the raid, Rohan receives a call from his District Collector, subtly hinting at the factory's owner being Mr. Jayant Singh, a powerful state cabinet minister and a key figure in the ruling party, known for his influence over transfers and postings. The Collector advises caution, suggesting a "less confrontational" approach. Rohan's family also expresses concern about potential repercussions if he acts against such a powerful person.

The child labourers, their families, the factory owner, the local community, the district administration, the judiciary, and the integrity of public service are all significant stakeholders. Rohan's decision will have profound implications for the children's future, the rule of law, the public perception of governance, and his own career trajectory.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Rohan Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
