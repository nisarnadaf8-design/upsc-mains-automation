Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Rahul Sharma, a young and earnest Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM) in the severely flood-affected district of Raigarh, is leading critical disaster relief and rehabilitation efforts. The recent flash floods have devastated thousands of families, leaving them without shelter, food, or medical aid. The state government has released substantial emergency funds, and Rahul is directly responsible for ensuring its transparent and efficient deployment for aid distribution.

During a routine audit of procurement records for emergency supplies, Rahul uncovers alarming discrepancies: significantly inflated bills for tents, medical kits, and food grains, along with questionable vendor choices. Evidence strongly suggests the involvement of Ms. Anjali Singh, the revered District Collector and his immediate senior, who has a reputation for integrity. Rahul is now confronted with a grave ethical dilemma: reporting this during an ongoing crisis could destabilize relief operations and damage public trust, while ignoring it means complicity in corruption that directly harms vulnerable flood victims.

The immediate stakeholders are the suffering flood victims whose very survival depends on the judicious use of funds, the public whose trust in governance is at stake, the integrity of the civil services, and Rahul’s own conscience and career. Both action and inaction carry severe personal and public consequences.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Rahul Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
