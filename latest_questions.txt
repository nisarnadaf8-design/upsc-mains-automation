Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Anjali Sharma, a diligent Deputy Commissioner in the State Treasury Department of Karnataka, recently uncovered a complex web of financial irregularities amounting to several crores in a public infrastructure project. Following due protocol, she meticulously documented her findings and officially reported the fraud to the Chief Secretary’s office, providing irrefutable evidence.

Soon after her report, Anjali began facing subtle but increasing retaliation. Her immediate superior, a close associate of a powerful political leader implicated in the scam, started assigning her to irrelevant tasks, isolating her, and initiating a dubious internal inquiry against her for alleged procedural lapses. Simultaneously, veiled threats concerning her family's safety reached her through anonymous channels, creating immense psychological pressure. She feels cornered between her duty to expose corruption and the severe risks to her personal life and family.

Anjali’s dilemma involves her personal safety, her family's well-being, her career trajectory, and the larger public interest in combating corruption. Her decision will have significant consequences for the integrity of the administration, the future of whistleblowers, and public trust in governance.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Anjali Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
