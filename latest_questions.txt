Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Rahul Sharma, a dedicated District Development Officer (DDO) in the Rural Development Ministry, is posted in Rudrapur District, a region severely affected by recurring droughts. He is currently compiling the mid-term impact assessment report for the central government’s flagship “Jal-Dhanya Samriddhi Yojana,” a critical welfare scheme aimed at water conservation and agricultural productivity enhancement. His team's preliminary ground surveys, however, reveal significant discrepancies: the scheme's on-ground performance is far below expected targets, plagued by implementation gaps and suspected diversion of funds.

As Rahul prepares the report, he faces immense pressure from the District Collector, his immediate superior, and a powerful local Member of Parliament (MP), who is a key proponent of the scheme. They insist that he inflate success metrics and manipulate key data points, arguing it is essential to secure continued funding, maintain public confidence, and avoid political embarrassment for the administration. Subtle hints about potential negative career ramifications, possibly affecting his family's stability, are also conveyed if he fails to comply.

Rahul's decision will directly impact thousands of impoverished farmers relying on the scheme, compromise the integrity of public data and governance, and either uphold or severely damage the credibility of his office and the government itself. Both complying with the pressure and adhering to ethical standards carry profound, long-term consequences for all involved.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Rahul Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
