Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Akshat Sharma, a newly posted Sub-Divisional Magistrate in the Mandla district, Madhya Pradesh, is known for his dedication and commitment to transparent public service. His current charge involves overseeing land acquisition processes for a proposed industrial corridor project, crucial for the region's economic development and attracting significant private investment.

During his detailed review, Akshat uncovers glaring discrepancies in land valuation reports and instances where prime land is being subtly diverted to a large private builder at undervalued rates, causing significant loss to marginal farmers. His immediate superior, the District Collector, subtly advises him to expedite clearances without "unnecessary scrutiny." An influential local political leader also pressures Akshat for the project's swift progression, hinting at severe "long-term career implications" if he obstructs it.

The affected stakeholders include hundreds of marginal farmers facing displacement, the private builder poised for undue profit, and the public trust in fair governance. Akshat's decision, whether to act on the irregularities or comply with pressure, will have profound consequences for his career, the affected community, and the ethical fabric of the administration.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
The ethical issues are conflict of interest (private gain vs. public good), injustice to marginal farmers, lack of transparency, administrative corruption, and erosion of public trust. Akshat faces a severe dilemma between upholding integrity, fulfilling public duty, and succumbing to political and hierarchical pressure.

(b) What are the options available to Akshat Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
Akshat's options include: (1) Comply with pressure: Merits - career safety, project continuity. Demerits - sacrifices integrity, injustice to farmers, erodes public trust. (2) Report internally: Merits - follows due process, attempts resolution within system. Demerits - risks suppression, retaliation, slow action. (3) Whistleblow externally: Merits - public exposure, justice for affected. Demerits - high personal risk, career jeopardy, institutional damage. (4) Meticulously document evidence and seek legal counsel: Merits - strengthens position, protects self. Demerits - time-consuming, no guaranteed success.

(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
I recommend Akshat Sharma meticulously document all discrepancies and formally raise concerns in writing to his immediate superior, citing specific rules and acquisition guidelines. If unaddressed, he should escalate the matter through proper hierarchical channels, like the Chief Secretary, concurrently seeking advice from trusted ethical seniors or legal counsel. This approach balances duty with self-preservation, utilizing institutional mechanisms before considering external options.

To handle pressures, he must remain professional, objective, and rely strictly on facts and evidence. He should avoid direct confrontation, instead presenting reasoned arguments for ethical governance. He must prepare for potential backlash, including transfers or isolation, by ensuring all his actions are legally sound and morally defensible. His primary allegiance must remain with constitutional values and public interest, using available whistleblower protection mechanisms if escalation becomes unavoidable.
