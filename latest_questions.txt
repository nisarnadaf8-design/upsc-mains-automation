Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Ms. Anjali Sharma, a diligent District Collector in a Scheduled V Area of Chhattisgarh, has been actively working to empower the particularly vulnerable Abujhmaria tribal community. She initiated successful livelihood projects based on sustainable forest produce and traditional farming, gaining immense trust and goodwill among the tribals, significantly improving their socio-economic status.

Recently, the State Government issued an order for a large-scale bauxite mining project in the heart of the forest, citing substantial revenue generation and industrial development for the state. This project would necessitate the displacement of several tribal villages and extensive deforestation in the very area where Ms. Sharma's projects are flourishing, directly threatening their traditional lands and way of life. She faces immense pressure from the Chief Secretary for rapid implementation, while the local MLA is publicly championing the project, subtly hinting at repercussions for any obstruction. Meanwhile, tribal elders, who view Ms. Sharma as their protector, are appealing to her to save their ancestral land.

Ms. Sharma is caught in a profound ethical dilemma. Complying with the state order would mean betraying the trust of a vulnerable community and negating her own efforts for their welfare, potentially leading to social unrest and environmental damage. Resisting or delaying the project would invite severe political and administrative censure, possibly jeopardizing her career and personal standing.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Ms. Anjali Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
