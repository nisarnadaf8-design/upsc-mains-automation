Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Avantika Sharma, a young and earnest IAS officer, serves as the Sub-Divisional Magistrate in Jagannathpur district, a coastal region in Odisha known for its vulnerability to natural disasters. Following a devastating super-cyclone that ravaged the district, she is at the forefront of coordinating extensive relief and rehabilitation efforts, overseeing distribution of essential supplies, medical camps, and temporary shelter construction.

During her field visits and review of procurement records, Avantika uncovers irrefutable evidence of large-scale irregularities in the allocation of contracts for temporary shelters and procurement of emergency relief materials, pointing towards active collusion and misappropriation of funds by her immediate superior, the District Collector, Mr. Rajeev Mehta. Simultaneously, the influential local MLA, whose family business incidentally secured some of these contracts, subtly advises Avantika to focus on 'expeditious delivery' rather than 'unnecessary scrutiny', hinting at potential career hurdles if she 'disrupts the established process'.

Avantika faces immense pressure to either ignore the corruption for the sake of perceived 'smooth operations' and career stability, or expose it, knowing it could tarnish the district administration's image, delay critical relief efforts temporarily, and potentially put her own career and safety at risk. The lives and immediate recovery of thousands of vulnerable cyclone victims hang in the balance, dependent on the integrity of the relief distribution system.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Avantika Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
