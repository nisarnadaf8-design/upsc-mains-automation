Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Ritu Sharma, a dynamic young IAS officer, is serving as the Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM) in a district recently devastated by severe floods. She is actively involved in coordinating relief and rehabilitation efforts, working under the direct supervision of Mr. Alok Verma, a senior and highly regarded IAS officer designated as the Special Relief Commissioner. The situation is chaotic, with immense pressure to provide immediate assistance to thousands of displaced people.

During a routine inspection of a relief camp and subsequent review of procurement records, Ritu observes glaring discrepancies in the quantity and quality of essential relief materials, specifically in the tendering process overseen by Mr. Verma's office. She also receives credible intelligence, hinting at a nexus between certain suppliers and a senior officer, suggesting the diversion of funds meant for the flood victims. Reporting this could severely disrupt the ongoing relief operations and potentially tarnish the reputation of a senior colleague known for his political connections.

The district administration is already under public scrutiny for slow relief efforts, and any internal conflict could further erode public trust and invite political interference. Ritu understands that a quiet dismissal of her observations could save her career from immediate friction, but the plight of the flood victims, whose lives depend on these resources, weighs heavily on her conscience. Both taking action and remaining silent have significant, far-reaching consequences for her, the victims, the administration, and the public.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Ritu Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
