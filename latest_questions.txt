Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Anjali Sharma, a diligent District Revenue Officer (DRO) in Mandla, Madhya Pradesh, has built a reputation for her integrity and adherence to rules since her recent posting. Her department is crucial for issuing various domicile, income, and caste certificates, which are often prerequisites for accessing government welfare schemes and electoral rolls.

With state assembly elections approaching, the powerful local MLA, Mr. Rajendra Singh, from the ruling party, approaches Anjali. He requests her to expedite and even backdate numerous income and residence certificates for his party's supporters, subtly implying these are crucial for their eligibility in upcoming government schemes designed to boost electoral prospects. Simultaneously, her immediate superior, a senior IAS officer, informally advises her to "maintain pragmatic relations" with political representatives for smooth district administration, hinting at potential career hurdles if she antagonizes them.

Anjali's decision will directly impact genuine beneficiaries, the fairness of electoral processes, public trust in the administration, her own professional integrity, and potentially the political stability of the region. Both compliance and refusal carry significant personal and systemic ramifications.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Anjali Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
