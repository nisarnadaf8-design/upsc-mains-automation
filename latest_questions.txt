Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Rakesh Sharma is the District Magistrate of a tribal-dominated district in a resource-rich region. He is tasked with overseeing the expedited implementation of the "Bharat Connect Highway Project," a crucial national infrastructure initiative designed to link remote areas and boost economic activity across several states. While the project promises significant national development and connectivity, a 30 km stretch of the proposed highway passes directly through a pristine forest region, affecting the ancestral lands and traditional livelihoods of the indigenous 'Vanvasi' community.

The Vanvasi community, supported by local environmental and tribal rights organizations, is vehemently opposing the project, citing irreversible loss of their cultural identity, ecological degradation, and inadequate rehabilitation proposals that do not account for their unique forest-based existence. Rakesh is under immense pressure from the Chief Secretary and the State Minister for Infrastructure to ensure the project stays on schedule, as any delay would incur substantial financial penalties and political repercussions. He believes in the necessity of development but is deeply conflicted by the potential displacement and cultural disruption faced by a vulnerable community.

The decision Rakesh makes will have profound consequences for the Vanvasi community's survival, the ecological balance of the region, the timely completion and economic viability of a national project, and his own professional integrity. Both immediate and long-term societal, environmental, and economic impacts are at stake, affecting not only the local populace but also the broader national interest.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Rakesh Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
