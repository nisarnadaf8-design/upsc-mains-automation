Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Ms. Anjali Sharma is an Under Secretary in the Ministry of Infrastructure Development in New Delhi, responsible for overseeing a crucial public-private partnership (PPP) project aimed at smart city development. The project, in its advanced stages, involves significant land acquisition and obtaining environmental clearances, with sensitive negotiations currently underway with various stakeholders.

A local environmental activist, Mr. Rajesh Kumar, files a Right to Information (RTI) application demanding immediate access to all project-related documents, including internal deliberations, preliminary legal opinions, and unfinalized draft reports pertaining to the Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA). Ms. Sharma's Joint Secretary firmly instructs her to withhold specific sections of this information under the Official Secrets Act, citing potential market speculation, public panic, and a law and order situation if premature data is disclosed. However, Mr. Kumar argues that withholding information compromises public trust and the fundamental right of citizens to be fully informed about projects impacting their environment and livelihood.

Ms. Sharma's decision will directly affect the project's timeline, the financial viability of the developers, the trust of local residents and environmental groups, and potentially her own career. Both full disclosure and complete withholding have serious ramifications, ranging from jeopardizing ongoing negotiations to accusations of opacity and institutional malpractice.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Ms. Anjali Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
