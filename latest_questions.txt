Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Rakesh Kumar, a decorated IPS officer known for his integrity and investigative acumen, was recently transferred to lead a special investigation unit within the State Anti-Corruption Bureau (ACB) in a prominent metropolitan city. His current mandate is to probe large-scale irregularities in public infrastructure projects that have faced significant delays and cost overruns.

During a high-profile investigation, Rakesh uncovers irrefutable evidence that a significant portion of the corruption in a major public works scam directly involves several senior officers within his own State Police Department, including some who previously held positions of power in the ACB. His immediate superior, a respected Additional Director General (ADG), subtly advises him to "handle the matter discreetly" due to the potential damage to the department's reputation and morale, and the risk of political fallout. A prominent political leader, whose associates are also implicated, issues veiled threats regarding Rakesh's career and personal safety. Meanwhile, civil society activists are demanding quick action, and the media is speculating about a cover-up.

Exposing the scam could lead to a massive institutional crisis, erode public trust in the police further, and jeopardize Rakesh's career and family's safety. Covering it up would compromise justice, encourage systemic corruption, and betray his oath of office and public trust, affecting millions of citizens.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case.
(b) What are the options available to Rakesh Kumar? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits.
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved?
