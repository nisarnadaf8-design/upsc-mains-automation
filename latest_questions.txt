Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Anjali Sharma, District Project Director for the flagship 'Gramin Utthan Yojana' in Shantipur District, has spent months meticulously overseeing the scheme's implementation. Her team has compiled a detailed report, indicating significant ground-level challenges and an achievement rate slightly below the ambitious targets due to genuine local issues like monsoon disruptions and supply chain bottlenecks.

However, just days before the report is due to be submitted to the state government, Ms. Sharma is called by her immediate superior, the Divisional Commissioner, who conveys "strong suggestions" from a powerful political minister. The minister expects the report to show 100% target achievement to ensure continued central funding and project expansion in the region. Ms. Sharma is subtly informed that failure to "adjust" the figures could jeopardize her career progression and even lead to a transfer to a less desirable posting. Simultaneously, some local activists, aware of the ground realities, are threatening to expose any falsification of data.

The decision Ms. Sharma makes will profoundly affect thousands of genuine beneficiaries who might be deprived of further aid based on manipulated data, the credibility of the government's welfare initiatives, and the integrity of her office. Both complying and refusing have serious personal and professional repercussions.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Anjali Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
