Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Ananya Singh, a diligent and upright IPS officer, holds the position of Superintendent of Police in the historically sensitive district of Avantika. Known for her commitment to transparency and justice, she has been instrumental in modernizing local police operations and enhancing community outreach over the past year.

During a routine internal audit initiated by Ananya following several public complaints, her team uncovered a sophisticated procurement scam involving inflated invoices and substandard equipment supply to the police department across the state, stretching back five years. The preliminary findings strongly implicate a former Inspector General of Police, who is now a respected political figure, and several high-ranking officers currently serving in various capacities, including some within Ananya's immediate circle and even her own superior. Ananya faces immense pressure from a powerful state minister to halt the investigation, who subtly reminds her of potential adverse impacts on her career and the "reputation of the force."

Ananya's decision in this matter will profoundly affect the credibility of the police force, the morale of honest officers, the lives of those implicated, and the trust citizens place in governance. Both pursuing and suppressing the investigation carry significant professional and personal consequences.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Ananya Singh? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
