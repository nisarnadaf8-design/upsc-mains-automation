Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Mr. Alok Kumar, a highly respected District Collector known for his integrity, is currently overseeing a crucial land acquisition project for a large industrial corridor in Sargam District. This project promises significant economic development but involves complex negotiations with thousands of landowners, requiring meticulous adherence to legal and ethical guidelines.

A critical ethical dilemma arises when Mr. Alok discovers that a substantial parcel of land belonging to his brother-in-law, Mr. Rajesh Kumar, falls directly within the acquisition zone. Rajesh stands to receive considerable compensation, which family members, including Alok's wife, are subtly pressuring him to 'expedite' and 'optimize'. Simultaneously, the Chief Secretary has stressed the importance of absolute transparency, while local farmer unions are closely monitoring the process for any hint of favoritism or malpractice.

Mr. Alok's decision will directly impact his personal reputation, family relations, the credibility of the entire district administration, the smooth progression of a vital state project, and the perception of justice among the affected landowners. Both direct intervention and perceived inaction carry severe implications for public trust and administrative efficacy.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Mr. Alok Kumar? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
