Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Anjali Sharma, a diligent Superintendent of Police (SP) in a prominent district, is leading an Anti-Corruption Bureau (ACB) investigation into widespread irregularities in a state government procurement project for infrastructure development. Known for her unblemished record and methodical approach, Anjali has recently garnered significant public trust in her efforts to tackle corruption.

During her meticulous investigation, Anjali uncovers substantial evidence pointing to a multi-crore scam involving collusion between a large construction firm and senior officials within her own State Police Housing Corporation, a wing responsible for departmental infrastructure projects. Further, initial findings suggest the implicit approval, if not direct involvement, of a high-ranking political functionary and a powerful superior officer within the broader police hierarchy, who has been a long-term mentor. She faces immense pressure from her immediate superior to "manage" the investigation discreetly to protect the department's image, while a local politician with vested interests subtly threatens her family's safety if she proceeds without compromise.

Anjali's decision will profoundly impact public trust in the police, the lives of honest officials and whistleblowers, the reputation of her department, and potentially her own career and family's well-being. Both exposing the truth and suppressing it carry severe implications for justice, accountability, and institutional integrity.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Anjali Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
