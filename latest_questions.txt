Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Ms. Ananya Sharma is a diligent District Magistrate (DM) of Rudrapur District in Uttar Pradesh. A major infrastructure project, a new highway bypassing a densely populated area, is in its final stages. The land acquisition process has been meticulously completed according to law, ensuring fair compensation and rehabilitation for all affected families.

The state's influential Minister for Public Works, Mr. Rajesh Singh, whose family owns a significant land parcel directly in the proposed highway's path, approaches Ms. Sharma. He demands a last-minute alteration to the highway alignment, citing minor environmental concerns, which would incidentally reroute the highway away from his land, increasing its value significantly. The Minister subtly threatens a transfer or adverse career implications if his demand is not met. Her immediate superior, the Divisional Commissioner, also advises "pragmatism" to avoid political friction.

Ms. Sharma's decision will directly affect the project's integrity, public trust, the environment, and the financial interests of both the affected original landholders and the Minister's family. Bending the rules would lead to costly delays and injustice, while upholding them could jeopardize her career.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Ms. Ananya Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
