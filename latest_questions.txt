Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Rakesh Kumar, a dedicated Superintendent of Police, is posted in Saharanpur, a district known for its diverse population and history of sporadic communal tensions. Recent weeks have seen escalating rhetoric and minor skirmishes following a local festival, putting the district administration on high alert for maintaining peace and order.

As tensions simmer, a significant incident of stone-pelting occurs between two communities. A powerful local MLA, with considerable political clout, directly contacts Rakesh, demanding immediate arrests of individuals exclusively from one community, irrespective of evidence, to send a 'strong message'. Simultaneously, his immediate superior, the District Magistrate, subtly advises him to consider 'political sensitivities' and avoid actions that might 'further complicate matters' with the ruling party, implying a need for compliance with the MLA's wishes.

Rakesh's decision will directly impact the lives of innocent citizens, the credibility of the police force, the fragile peace in the district, and his own professional integrity. Both immediate compliance and strict adherence to law carry significant, potentially volatile, consequences.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Rakesh Kumar? Evaluate each option with its merits and and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
