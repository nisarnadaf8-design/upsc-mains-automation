Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Mr. Arjun Sharma, a dynamic and upright IAS officer, serves as the District Magistrate of Shahpur District, Uttar Pradesh. Known for his commitment to grassroots development and social justice, he was conducting a surprise inspection of industrial units in the newly designated Special Economic Zone (SEZ) to assess compliance with labor laws and environmental norms.

During an inspection of 'Grand Textiles Pvt. Ltd.', a prominent garment factory, Mr. Sharma’s team discovered several children, visibly underage, working in hazardous conditions on the production floor. Further investigation revealed that the factory is owned by Mr. Raghavendra Singh, a powerful state cabinet minister and a key political figure in the ruling party, known for his influence across the district. Mr. Singh immediately contacts Mr. Sharma, subtly reminding him of his career prospects and the potential 'misunderstanding' that could damage both their reputations, urging him to 'handle the matter discreetly'.

Mr. Sharma's decision will directly affect the exploited children, the factory owner and his political career, his own professional integrity, the district administration's credibility, and potentially spark public outcry or political backlash. Both decisive action and passive inaction carry significant professional and personal repercussions.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Mr. Arjun Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
