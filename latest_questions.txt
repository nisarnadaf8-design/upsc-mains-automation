Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Anjali Sharma, a diligent District Magistrate in Shahjahanpur, is known for her impeccable integrity and efficient administration. Her office is currently processing a critical land acquisition dispute involving an ancestral property claim, which has been a contentious issue in the district for years.

Recently, Anjali discovered that her paternal uncle, who has a significant political presence in the region, is one of the primary claimants in the land dispute case currently awaiting her final administrative review. Her family insists that she fast-track the decision in their favour, citing family loyalty. Simultaneously, a local political leader, with vested interests in the opposing party, has subtly threatened to raise questions about her impartiality if she doesn't recuse herself or delay the process.

The decision will profoundly affect numerous landless families who have built their homes on parts of the disputed land, the public's trust in the fairness of the district administration, and Anjali’s personal reputation. Both expediting or delaying the case, or a decision in any direction, carry significant ethical and social consequences.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Anjali Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
