Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Rohan Mehta, an honest and diligent officer, serves as the Assistant Director in the Department of Public Works (DPW) in the bustling city of Rudrapur. For several months, he noticed discrepancies in the procurement processes for a major urban infrastructure project, indicating potential financial fraud amounting to several crores. Following proper channels, Rohan meticulously documented his findings and submitted a detailed report to the Chief Secretary’s office.

Instead of an investigation, Rohan soon finds himself sidelined; his key responsibilities are reassigned, and his integrity is questioned through anonymous complaints circulated within the department. His immediate superior, Mr. Sharma, advises him to retract his report for the sake of 'departmental harmony' and warns of potential harm to his career progression. Simultaneously, Rohan's family expresses concern for his safety and future, urging him to reconsider his stance, while a powerful local political leader, whose associates are implicated in Rohan’s report, begins to exert indirect pressure through veiled threats.

Rohan's decision will directly impact public trust in governance, the financial health of the state, the morale of honest officers, and his own career and family well-being. Both pursuing his complaint and yielding to pressure carry significant, far-reaching consequences for himself and the integrity of the public service.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Rohan Mehta? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
