Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Anjali Sharma, a diligent District Nodal Officer for the 'Gram Samridhi Awaas Yojana' (GSAY) under the Ministry of Rural Development, has been posted in the resource-scarce Ramgarh District of Bihar for the past two years. Her primary responsibility involves overseeing the scheme's implementation, monitoring beneficiary selection, and preparing quarterly progress reports detailing its impact on the rural poor.

As the deadline for the crucial annual impact assessment report approaches, Anjali receives a directive from the Principal Secretary of her department, heavily influenced by the local Member of Legislative Assembly (MLA), to 'adjust' the data. Specifically, she is asked to inflate the number of successful housing completions and exaggerate the income generation figures for beneficiaries, despite her ground surveys indicating only moderate progress due to various logistical and funding challenges. This manipulation is intended to ensure continued central funding and boost the MLA's political standing.

Anjali faces an ethical dilemma where complying would mean compromising her professional integrity and misleading the public and policymakers, while refusing could invite administrative repercussions, including a transfer or stalled career progression. Her decision will directly impact thousands of deserving rural families, the credibility of the welfare scheme, and the overall transparency of governance.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Anjali Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
