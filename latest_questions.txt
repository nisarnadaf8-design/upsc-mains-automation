Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Mr. Divya Prakash, a conscientious IAS officer, is serving as the District Magistrate in Sagarpur, a region recently devastated by unprecedented floods. He is directly responsible for the allocation and oversight of a substantial sum of INR 75 crores in emergency relief funds, crucial for immediate housing, food, and medical aid to hundreds of thousands of displaced individuals. His department has meticulously identified the most vulnerable villages and allocated resources based on urgent needs assessments.

Just as the relief operations gain momentum, Mr. Prakash receives an urgent call from a prominent Member of Parliament from an adjacent, less-affected district. The MP, a senior figure in the ruling party, strongly 'advises' Mr. Prakash to divert 15% of Sagarpur's funds to a few key villages in his constituency, promising expedited promotions and crucial political support for Mr. Prakash's future career. Simultaneously, a powerful local industrialist, whose properties were affected in the MP's constituency, offers a generous 'donation' to Mr. Prakash's family trust if the funds are redirected.

Mr. Prakash is acutely aware that diverting these funds would leave several severely impacted Sagarpur villages without crucial aid, potentially leading to further suffering and loss of life. His refusal, however, could result in immediate political retaliation, including a punitive transfer or departmental inquiry, jeopardizing his career and ability to serve.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Mr. Divya Prakash? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
