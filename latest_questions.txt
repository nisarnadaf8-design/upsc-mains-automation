Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Rajesh Sharma, a highly regarded IPS officer with a stellar record of integrity and public service, has recently taken charge as the Senior Superintendent of Police (SSP) in Durjanpur District, a region grappling with persistent law and order issues. Known for his methodical approach, he initiated a comprehensive review of past cases and departmental procurement processes within weeks of his posting.

During this review, Sharma uncovered irrefutable evidence of a sophisticated procurement scam involving ghost firms and inflated invoices for police equipment, siphoning off significant public funds. The investigation unequivocally pointed towards the involvement of a powerful sitting MLA and shockingly, a former Inspector General of Police (IGP) who was his direct senior and mentor earlier in his career. His current Director General of Police (DGP) subtly advised him to 'handle the matter discreetly' to avoid 'unnecessary departmental embarrassment' and hinted at potential 'career repercussions' if he proceeded with full transparency.

Rajesh now faces a profound ethical dilemma. His decision will significantly impact the victims of the scam (taxpayers), the morale and public trust in the entire police force, the careers of implicated officers, his own professional future, and potentially the stability of the state government. Both exposing the truth and suppressing it carry serious and far-reaching consequences.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Rajesh Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
