Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Ms. Anjali Sharma, a young and upright District Magistrate of Rudraprayag district, Uttarakhand, has recently taken charge amidst an ongoing ambitious highway expansion project aimed at improving connectivity to remote pilgrimage sites. Her initial reviews revealed several irregularities in land acquisition proposals and environmental impact assessments for a critical section.

A powerful local Member of Legislative Assembly (MLA), Mr. Rakesh Verma, has approached Ms. Sharma, insisting on a rapid approval for a particular stretch of the highway that involves acquiring a land parcel owned by his close associates at an inflated price, bypassing due diligence. Her immediate superior, the Chief Secretary, subtly hinted at "political sensitivities" and advised a "pragmatic approach" to avoid confrontation, while the MLA himself suggested potential career repercussions for non-cooperation.

This dilemma directly impacts the marginalized villagers whose legitimate land claims might be undervalued, the ecological balance of the sensitive Himalayan region, the integrity of the public exchequer, and the credibility of the administrative machinery. Ms. Sharma's decision carries significant implications for social justice, environmental sustainability, and her own professional future.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Ms. Anjali Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
