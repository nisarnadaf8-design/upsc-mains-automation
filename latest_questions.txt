Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Ms. Aditi Sharma, District Project Coordinator for the Disaster Management Unit in Sundergarh, Odisha, is overseeing the distribution of crucial relief funds following unprecedented monsoon floods. The allocated funds, amounting to several crores, are earmarked for immediate shelter, food, and medical supplies for thousands of displaced families in remote, inaccessible villages. Her team has been working tirelessly to ensure fair and timely disbursement.

Amidst the crisis, Ms. Sharma receives an urgent call from a senior political leader from the ruling party, requesting a significant portion of the relief funds be immediately diverted to a constituency not directly affected by the floods but crucial for an upcoming by-election. Simultaneously, her immediate superior subtly advises her to 'cooperate' for the sake of 'smoother operations' and her career progression. Delay in relief would exacerbate the suffering in Sundergarh, while defiance risks political backlash and potential administrative harassment.

The primary stakeholders are the flood-affected victims of Sundergarh who face starvation and disease if funds are diverted, and the public whose trust in governance would erode. Diverting funds would also benefit political expediency, while refusal could lead to negative consequences for Ms. Sharma's career and potentially disrupt critical disaster response efforts.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
The core ethical issues are public interest versus political pressure, integrity versus careerism, duty of care towards vulnerable citizens, transparency in public funds, and maintaining administrative impartiality in a crisis situation.

(b) What are the options available to Ms. Aditi Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
1. Divert funds as requested: Merits - avoids political wrath, potential for career advancement. Demerits - profound ethical violation, illegal, causes immense suffering for victims, betrays public trust.
2. Refuse and inform superior formally: Merits - upholds integrity, protects public funds. Demerits - potential for transfer, stalled career, political pressure, administrative backlash, and potential for the superior to still proceed.
3. Report the matter to higher authorities/vigilance: Merits - promotes systemic accountability, protects victims and rule of law. Demerits - risk to personal safety, isolation, prolonged investigation, potential for political vendetta against her.
4. Seek written orders for fund diversion: Merits - creates a paper trail, formalizes accountability. Demerits - might not be issued, still involves a potentially illegal act, could delay legitimate relief.

(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
I would recommend Ms. Sharma unequivocally refuse to divert the funds. Her primary duty is to the flood victims and the principles of public service, integrity, and rule of law.
To handle the intense pressures, she should:
1. Document everything: Request the diversion order in writing from both the political leader and her superior, stating the specific reasons for denial (e.g., legal framework for disaster funds).
2. Uphold rules: Firmly state that the funds are allocated by law for specific relief purposes and diversion is illegal and unethical, citing relevant rules.
3. Consult and inform: Discreetly seek advice from trusted senior colleagues or legal experts within the administration. If pressure persists, she must escalate the matter to the Chief Secretary or the state vigilance department, providing documented evidence.
4. Transparency: If internal channels fail and her position becomes untenable, she could consider making the issue public to ensure accountability and protect the funds, but only as a last resort. This approach protects her integrity and ensures justice for the victims.
