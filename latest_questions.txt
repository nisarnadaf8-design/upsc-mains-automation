Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Rajesh Kumar, a diligent officer known for his integrity, recently took charge as the District Environment Officer in a rapidly developing industrial hub of a coastal state. His department, the State Pollution Control Board, is crucial for monitoring industrial compliance. A new, state-of-the-art textile processing unit, GreenWeave Textiles, has just commenced operations, heralded as a major foreign investment promising significant local employment.

During a routine inspection, Rajesh’s team discovered clear evidence of GreenWeave Textiles bypassing its effluent treatment plant, discharging untreated dyes and chemicals into a nearby river, a lifeline for several downstream villages. As Rajesh prepared to issue a show-cause notice, he received a call from his Chief Engineer, suggesting a "pragmatic approach" given the unit's economic importance and political backing. Shortly after, the local MLA personally intervened, highlighting the unit's employment generation and the negative impact of any immediate punitive action on investor sentiment. A representative from the industry even subtly offered "expedited processing" for future approvals and "personal consideration" for Rajesh.

Rajesh’s decision will directly impact the health and livelihoods of the downstream communities, the ecological balance of the river, and the long-term credibility of the State Pollution Control Board. It also carries significant implications for GreenWeave Textiles' operations, local employment, and future industrial investments in the region, while also shaping Rajesh’s own professional integrity and career trajectory.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Rajesh Kumar? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
