Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Ms. Anjali Sharma, a diligent District Collector, is posted in the flood-ravaged district of Raigarh, known for its challenging terrain and frequent natural disasters. Following unprecedented monsoon floods in July 2026 that displaced thousands and caused widespread destruction, the central and state governments released substantial relief funds. As the officer in-charge of the disaster relief and rehabilitation operations, Ms. Sharma is directly responsible for the transparent and timely disbursement of these critical funds to the affected population.

During a high-level review meeting, the Chief Secretary subtly conveyed expectations to Ms. Sharma about contributing a portion of the relief funds towards an "urgent state infrastructure project" championed by the ruling party, hinting at career implications for non-compliance. Concurrently, the influential local MLA, Mr. Rajeev Mehta, openly pressed her to approve inflated tenders from specific contractors for temporary shelters, threatening public protests and her transfer if his demands were not met. Ms. Sharma also senses an unstated pressure from some subordinates, who refer to "flexible allocation practices" during previous crises.

Ms. Sharma is acutely aware that any diversion of funds would directly deprive vulnerable flood victims of essential aid, intensifying their suffering and potentially leading to starvation or further loss of life. Her decision will significantly impact the trust citizens place in governance, the moral standing of her administration, and her personal integrity, with both compliance and defiance having severe ramifications.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Ms. Anjali Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
