Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Anjali Sharma, a diligent and upright District Magistrate of Kalinganagar district, is known for her impeccable record in public service. Her district is grappling with rapid urbanization, necessitating significant infrastructure upgrades. Her current major responsibility involves overseeing the environmental impact assessment and granting final clearances for a crucial multi-crore highway bypass project, designed to alleviate severe traffic congestion and boost regional connectivity.

A challenging situation arises when it becomes known that 'EcoBuild Corp', a leading construction firm owned by her elder brother, Rajesh Sharma, has emerged as the frontrunner for securing the bypass project contract. This project falls directly under Anjali's jurisdictional purview for various local permits and clearances. Her family exerts subtle pressure to expedite the process, while a local environmental activist group alleges potential conflict of interest and threatens public protests if due process is compromised.

Her decision will significantly impact the district's development trajectory, the reputation of her office, the environmental well-being of the region, the financial interests of her family, and public trust in administration. Both acting directly or recusing herself carries weighty professional and personal consequences.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Anjali Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
