Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Dr. Rhea Sharma, a proactive District Magistrate in Rudrapur district, Uttarakhand, was overseeing critical flood relief operations following unprecedented torrential rains. Her team was tirelessly working to provide essential supplies, temporary shelters, and medical aid to thousands displaced by the disaster. The situation demanded immediate and transparent resource allocation to prevent further loss of life and suffering.

During a routine audit of supply chains, Dr. Sharma unearthed strong evidence of a major diversion of relief materials, including food grains, medicines, and tarpaulins, intended for the worst-affected villages. The evidence pointed directly to her immediate superior, the Divisional Commissioner, who had previously warned her against "unnecessary scrutiny" to maintain operational speed. A local political leader, whose constituency was meant to receive some of the diverted aid, also subtly hinted at "cooperation" to avoid disrupting the fragile relief efforts.

Dr. Sharma's decision will directly impact the lives of thousands of vulnerable flood victims who desperately await aid, potentially leading to widespread suffering and loss of life if resources are not received. Her actions could either expose corruption, potentially saving lives and ensuring justice, or shield a powerful official, leading to further suffering and eroding public trust in governance during a critical humanitarian crisis.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Dr. Rhea Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
