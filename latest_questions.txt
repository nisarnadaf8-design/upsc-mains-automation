Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Divya Sharma, a diligent District Forest Officer (DFO) in the ecologically sensitive district of Suryanagar, Karnataka, has recently been tasked with overseeing the demarcation of an eco-sensitive zone (ESZ) boundary around a wildlife sanctuary. Her reputation for strict adherence to environmental regulations and fair implementation of rules is well-known.

A powerful state minister, Mr. Sudhir Gowda, holding extensive land adjacent to the proposed ESZ, pressures Divya to re-align the boundary, excluding a significant portion of his property earmarked for a resort. The minister’s aide explicitly assures 'uninterrupted career progression' for Divya if she complies. Concurrently, her immediate superior, the Conservator of Forests, subtly advises her to ‘exercise administrative flexibility’ to avoid ‘political friction’ and maintain a harmonious working relationship with influential persons.

Divya's decision will directly impact the ecological integrity of the wildlife sanctuary, public trust in environmental governance, and the livelihood of local communities. Both compliance and defiance carry serious, far-reaching consequences for her career and the broader administrative ethos.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Divya Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
