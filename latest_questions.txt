Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
SP Abhishek Singh, recently transferred to Sitapur District as its Police Chief, is known for his integrity and proactive approach. During a routine internal audit and intelligence gathering exercise initiated by him to streamline department functioning, his team uncovers discrepancies in procurement records for critical police infrastructure projects over the last two years. The financial irregularities seem to indicate a systemic misappropriation of funds.

Abhishek discovers these irregularities point to a well-entrenched network involving senior officers within the district police department, including his immediate predecessor and possibly some current colleagues. Furthermore, a highly influential local politician, known to be close to the state's ruling party, appears to have facilitated several of these dubious contracts. His immediate superior, the Inspector General (IG) of Police, advises caution, citing potential damage to the department's reputation and morale, and warns of severe political backlash that could jeopardise his own career.

Exposing the scam could lead to internal turmoil, political instability, and threats to his safety, but inaction would perpetuate corruption, erode public trust, and compromise justice. Both paths carry significant personal and institutional risks.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Abhishek Singh? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
