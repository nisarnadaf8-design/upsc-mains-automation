Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Ms. Priya Singh, a newly appointed IAS officer, is serving as the Sub-Divisional Magistrate of a remote, flood-devastated region in Assam. Following a catastrophic monsoon, her primary responsibility is to oversee the distribution of emergency relief supplies and rehabilitation efforts for thousands of displaced villagers. The scale of the disaster has brought immense pressure to deliver aid swiftly and transparently.

During a surprise inspection of a relief warehouse, Priya uncovers irrefutable evidence that her immediate superior, Mr. Alok Verma, a respected and politically well-connected District Collector with whom she shares a good working relationship, is orchestrating the diversion of high-value relief materials for personal gain. Verma has built a strong network of patronage over the years, and previous complaints against him have been discreetly suppressed. Priya realises that confronting him could not only jeopardise her nascent career but also potentially disrupt the ongoing, critical relief operations, while ignoring it would mean complicity in grave corruption affecting vulnerable lives.

Priya's decision will have significant repercussions for the flood victims who are dependent on the diverted aid, the morale of honest officers involved in relief work, the reputation of the administration, and her own professional integrity. Both action and inaction carry severe ethical and practical consequences.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Ms. Priya Singh? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
