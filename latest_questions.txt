Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Ms. Aanya Sharma, a recently posted Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM) in District Rudrapur, is keen to establish a reputation for fairness and efficiency. She is overseeing the land acquisition process for a crucial industrial corridor project, a high priority for the state government aimed at boosting regional employment and investment.

During her review, Aanya uncovers disturbing irregularities where prime agricultural land, intended for public utility, is being acquired at significantly undervalued rates. Initial inquiries suggest a nexus between local revenue officials, a powerful private real estate conglomerate, and a politically influential local MLA. Her immediate superior, the District Collector, subtly hints at the need for "smooth execution" and avoiding "unnecessary delays or complications" when Aanya raises her preliminary concerns.

Thousands of marginal farmers stand to lose their land and livelihood without fair compensation, threatening to erupt into widespread protests. The integrity of the land acquisition process itself, along with public trust in the administration, is at stake. Aanya realises that both action and inaction carry severe personal and professional consequences.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Ms. Aanya Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
