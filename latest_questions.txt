Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Rahul Sharma, a young and dynamic District Collector in the tribal-dominated Ramgarh district of Odisha, has recently been posted with the primary mandate to expedite a major national highway project. This project is deemed critical for national economic integration and has received clearances at the highest levels. However, its route necessitates the acquisition of significant tracts of ancestral land belonging to several indigenous communities, whose rights are protected under the Fifth Schedule and PESA Act.

The communities are vehemently opposing displacement, citing deep cultural ties to their land, imminent loss of traditional livelihoods, and inadequate rehabilitation packages proposed by the project authority. Local environmental and tribal rights activists have mobilized significant public opinion against the project, alleging a nexus between the implementing agency and local political figures. Rahul's Divisional Commissioner has explicitly instructed him to proceed with land acquisition swiftly, highlighting the project's national importance and the need to avoid any delays, while senior political leaders are pressuring for immediate action.

Rahul's decision will directly affect the livelihoods and cultural identity of thousands of indigenous people, the reputation of the state government, and the timely completion of a national infrastructure project. Both forceful acquisition and significant delays carry serious consequences for his career, public trust, and social stability in the region.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Rahul Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
