Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Manoj Kumar is a District Land Records Officer in Purnia district, Bihar, known for his unblemished record of integrity. His department is currently verifying land ownership and issuing certificates for a new state-sponsored housing project aimed at economically weaker sections. This scheme promises significant relief to many impoverished families.

A senior political leader, the sitting MLA from the ruling party, approaches Manoj with a direct demand. The leader insists that Manoj issue several 'occupancy certificates' for specific land parcels in a contested area to individuals who do not legally own them. These certificates are crucial for them to qualify for the housing scheme. The leader clearly states that these individuals are key party workers whose loyalty depends on receiving this benefit, hinting at severe career repercussions for Manoj if he refuses. His immediate superior, under palpable political pressure, also subtly advises Manoj to "find a way" to accommodate the request to avoid further complications.

Manoj is acutely aware that issuing false certificates would not only violate established legal procedures but also deprive genuine beneficiaries and set a dangerous precedent. His decision will affect his reputation, the credibility of his office, the political leader's influence, and the trust of the public in the fair execution of government schemes.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Manoj Kumar? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
