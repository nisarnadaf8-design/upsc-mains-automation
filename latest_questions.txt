Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Manoj Kumar, a highly regarded District Magistrate of Aridpur District, is known for his impartiality and efficient handling of administrative affairs. He is currently overseeing a time-sensitive land acquisition process for a crucial national highway project designed to significantly boost regional connectivity and economic activity in a historically underdeveloped region.

A substantial parcel of land required for the highway alignment belongs to his paternal uncle, Mr. Suresh Kumar, a prominent local landlord. Mr. Suresh is vehemently resisting the acquisition, claiming sentimental value for the ancestral property and disputing the proposed compensation rates. Manoj faces immense pressure from his Principal Secretary to expedite the acquisition. Simultaneously, the local MLA has made subtle inquiries, while Manoj's own family members have personally appealed to him to safeguard his uncle's interests, creating a significant conflict.

Manoj's decision will directly impact his family, the other landowners awaiting fair compensation, the reputation of the district administration, and the timely completion of a vital public infrastructure project. Both an overly lenient approach and an uncompromising stance carry significant financial, social, and ethical consequences, potentially leading to public outcry or accusations of conflict of interest.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Manoj Kumar? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
