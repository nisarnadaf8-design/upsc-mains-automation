========================================

Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Manoj Kumar, an upright and highly decorated IPS officer, was recently posted as the Superintendent of Police, heading the State Police Cyber Crime Unit in a major metropolitan city. Known for his keen investigative skills and unwavering commitment to justice, he had successfully streamlined the unit's operations, leading to several high-profile arrests in recent months.

His unit recently uncovered a sophisticated online recruitment scam targeting thousands of unemployed youth for government positions. As the investigation deepened, preliminary evidence revealed the shocking involvement of a few mid-level officers from the state police's own recruitment wing, alongside covert support from a powerful political functionary. Manoj's immediate superior subtly advised him to proceed with extreme caution, emphasizing the potential for severe damage to public trust in the entire police force, while a prominent political leader suggested discreetly managing the "sensitive internal matter" to avoid departmental upheaval.

Manoj faces a profound ethical dilemma. His decision will directly impact the scam victims awaiting justice, the integrity and reputation of the police department, the political establishment, and potentially his own career and personal safety. Both exposing the truth and attempting to suppress it carry immense consequences for various stakeholders.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Manoj Kumar? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
