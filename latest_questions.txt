Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Ms. Avantika Sharma, an upright District Collector of the remote, resource-rich district of Janajeevani, has been overseeing the implementation of a major infrastructure project – a multi-purpose dam – deemed critical for regional development by the state government. The project requires the acquisition of a significant tract of forest land, traditionally inhabited by the vulnerable 'Dongri' tribal community, who hold ancestral rights to the land.

Recently, the State Chief Minister's office has instructed Ms. Sharma to expedite the land acquisition process and resettlement of the Dongri community within an unprecedented six-month deadline, citing national strategic importance and impending central funding expiration. However, her ground assessments, supported by local NGOs and community leaders, indicate that the proposed rehabilitation package is inadequate, the alternative land unsuitable, and forced relocation would severely disrupt their traditional way of life, social cohesion, and unique cultural practices, potentially leading to widespread resentment and social unrest. A powerful state minister is personally monitoring the progress and has conveyed displeasure at any delays.

The decision will profoundly affect thousands of Dongri tribals, impacting their livelihood, identity, and future generations. On the other hand, delaying the project risks loss of crucial state funding, political backlash from the government, and may be perceived as hindering vital development, leading to criticism from other local stakeholders who stand to benefit from the dam.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
The ethical issues involve balancing tribal welfare and rights against state development mandates, maintaining administrative integrity amidst political pressure, ensuring empathetic and just rehabilitation, and upholding impartiality in decision-making when faced with competing interests.

(b) What are the options available to Ms. Avantika Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
Option 1: Strictly follow state orders. Merits: Avoids political backlash, ensures project continuity. Demerits: Violates tribal rights, risks social unrest, compromises ethical governance, fuels injustice.
Option 2: Resolutely resist state orders, prioritize tribal rights. Merits: Upholds constitutional values, protects vulnerable. Demerits: Risks insubordination, career repercussions, potential project cancellation.
Option 3: Propose a revised, comprehensive rehabilitation plan. Merits: Seeks a just, sustainable solution, balances development with welfare, demonstrates leadership. Demerits: Requires skillful negotiation, may still face resistance and time constraints.

(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
I would recommend Option 3: Ms. Sharma should formally communicate her concerns to the Chief Minister's office, providing a detailed, evidence-based report highlighting the deficiencies in the current rehabilitation package and the potential long-term socio-cultural costs of forced relocation. She should concurrently propose an enhanced, culturally sensitive, and legally compliant rehabilitation and resettlement plan, clearly outlining its feasibility and benefits.

To handle pressures, she must maintain meticulous documentation of all communications and assessments. She should proactively engage with tribal representatives and state officials, acting as a facilitator to find common ground. Leveraging support from constitutional bodies or expert committees on tribal affairs could also strengthen her position. Her approach must be firm in principle, but collaborative in execution, demonstrating dedication to both public welfare and institutional integrity.
