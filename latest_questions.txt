GS-1 SOCIETY: Urbanisation: Smart Cities, Slums and Urban Governance
========================================

Q1. (10 Marks | 150 words)
Examine the extent to which India's Smart Cities Mission, despite its technological ambitions, has genuinely addressed the challenges of social equity and environmental resilience in rapidly expanding urban landscapes, beyond mere infrastructure upgrades.

Q2. (10 Marks | 150 words)
Critically examine how the symbiotic relationship between a burgeoning informal economy, a reactive urban planning approach, and insufficient housing policies perpetuates slum proliferation rather than facilitating comprehensive rehabilitation in Indian cities.

Q3. (15 Marks | 250 words)
Critically analyse the efficacy of the 74th Constitutional Amendment Act in empowering urban local bodies to effectively manage urban services, particularly solid waste and water challenges, in the face of rapid urbanisation. Discuss the political, financial, and administrative impediments that continue to hinder true decentralisation and sustainable urban governance.

Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Ms. Anjali Sharma, a diligent Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM) in a district known for its historical communal sensitivity in Uttar Pradesh, is widely respected for her integrity and efficient crisis management. A major local festival, often a flashpoint for tensions, is scheduled to commence in the coming week.

Following a minor altercation between two community groups during a preparatory procession, a prominent political leader from the ruling party, known for his inflammatory rhetoric, exerts immense pressure on Ms. Sharma. He demands that she issue prohibitory orders selectively targeting one community, effectively preventing their participation in a key ritualistic component of the upcoming festival. Her immediate superior, under similar political duress, discreetly advises her to "consider the larger political stability and prevailing sentiments" and comply, while local community leaders from the targeted group appeal to her for impartial treatment and protection of their fundamental rights.

Ms. Sharma's decision will directly impact public order, communal harmony, the fundamental rights of a significant population segment, her professional integrity, and the public's trust in impartial administration. Both compliance with political demands and adherence to ethical principles carry serious consequences for maintaining peace and for her own career trajectory.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Ms. Anjali Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
