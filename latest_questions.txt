Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Ms. Ananya Sharma is a young and diligent Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM) in the fictional district of Kailashpur, known for its socio-cultural diversity but also underlying communal sensitivities. For the past six months, she has been instrumental in maintaining peace through proactive community engagement and impartial enforcement of law and order. Recently, tensions have escalated in a specific locality following a minor dispute between two community groups that has been fueled by misinformation.

As Ananya prepares to deploy preventative measures and conduct peace committee meetings, she receives direct instructions from a powerful state minister, her political master, to take a decisively biased stance against one particular community. The minister, seeking to consolidate his vote bank, explicitly asks her to selectively apply crowd control measures and register FIRs disproportionately. Her immediate superior, the District Magistrate, subtly advises her to "manage the situation carefully, keeping in mind the political realities."

Ananya's decision will directly impact the lives and safety of thousands of citizens, the fragile communal harmony in Kailashpur, her professional integrity and career, and the credibility of the administration. Both strict adherence to impartiality and compliance with political directives carry significant and potentially volatile consequences.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Ms. Ananya Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
