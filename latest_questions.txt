Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Anya Sharma, a young and idealistic IAS officer, has recently been posted as the Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM) in District Palwal, Haryana. Keen to make a positive impact, she is overseeing a major land acquisition project for an ambitious industrial corridor, involving significant tracts of agricultural land from numerous small farmers. This project is touted as crucial for regional economic development.

During her review, Anya discovers several discrepancies in land valuation and ownership records. Her initial investigation suggests that prime land parcels, initially designated for public infrastructure, are being subtly diverted to a powerful private real estate conglomerate at drastically undervalued rates through manipulated revenue records. This manipulation appears to involve significant kickbacks to local political figures and some senior officials. Her immediate superior, the District Collector, hints at 'expediting' the process to avoid 'unnecessary delays' that could displease the state government. A prominent local political leader also contacts her, emphasizing the 'economic benefits' of the project and subtly reminding her of her nascent career trajectory.

The farmers stand to lose their land with inadequate compensation, the public exchequer is poised to bear significant losses, and the integrity of public administration is at stake. If Anya acts decisively, she faces potential career setbacks, a punitive transfer, or even threats. If she chooses inaction, she becomes complicit in a corrupt scheme, undermining justice and public trust.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Anya Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
