Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Rohan Sharma, a diligent District Project Coordinator in the Rural Development Department of Mandla District, Madhya Pradesh, has been instrumental in implementing a new tribal welfare scheme. The scheme provides skill development training and certification to eligible beneficiaries, enabling them to secure employment and improve their livelihoods. The district, predominantly tribal, is gearing up for crucial state assembly elections.

Just weeks before the elections, Rohan's immediate superior, the Additional District Magistrate, along with a prominent local political leader from the ruling party, approaches him with an unusual demand. They instruct him to expedite the issuance of several hundred skill development completion certificates to individuals who have not completed the requisite training or, in some cases, are not even eligible for the scheme. The stated reason is to showcase the scheme's 'success' to the electorate and consolidate support for the upcoming elections. Rohan is implicitly warned about potential career repercussions if he does not comply.

Rohan's decision will directly affect the integrity of the welfare scheme, the trust of genuine beneficiaries, and the overall credibility of the district administration. Compliance would benefit the political party electorally but compromise his ethical obligations, while refusal could jeopardize his professional standing and invite punitive transfers.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Rohan Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
