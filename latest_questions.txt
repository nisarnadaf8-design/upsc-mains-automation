========================================

Q4. (10 Marks | 150 words)
What do you understand by 'institutional integrity' in the context of public administration? Explain its significance in fostering public trust and ensuring probity in governance, particularly when faced with pressures that compromise transparency and accountability.

Q5. (10 Marks | 150 words)
Examine the ethical challenges a civil servant faces in identifying and managing potential conflicts of interest, both perceived and actual, within their official duties. How can a robust ethical framework and personal integrity help navigate these situations effectively?

Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Mr. Alok Sharma, a dedicated District Social Welfare Officer in Rudrapur district, Uttarakhand, is tasked with compiling the annual impact report for 'Poshan Shakti Abhiyan', a flagship central government scheme aimed at reducing malnutrition in children and pregnant women. The scheme's performance is crucial for future funding allocations and his district's reputation.

The District Magistrate (DM), under implicit pressure from the local Member of Parliament (MP) who is keen to showcase scheme success before an upcoming election, instructs Alok to 'optimise' (read: inflate) the beneficiary coverage and positive impact statistics in the report. Alok's field surveys and ground realities, however, indicate significant gaps in implementation and actual outcomes, suggesting that the scheme has only met about 60% of its targets. Manipulating the data would ensure the district looks good, securing more funds and avoiding political scrutiny, but would misrepresent the true state of affairs.

His decision will directly affect the malnourished beneficiaries who truly need intervention, the integrity of government reporting, future policy decisions based on flawed data, and his own conscience and career. Both compliance and defiance carry significant personal and professional risks.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
This case involves ethical issues of probity, transparency, accountability, and integrity in governance. It highlights the conflict between loyalty to superiors/political pressure and public interest, demanding honesty, objectivity, and courage from the civil servant in the face of misuse of authority.

(b) What are the options available to Mr. Alok Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
1. Comply and manipulate data: Merits include avoiding conflict, protecting career. Demerits involve compromising integrity, harming beneficiaries, perpetuating inefficiency, and undermining public trust and future policymaking.
2. Submit true report: Merits include upholding probity, transparency, and public interest, ensuring accurate data for policy correction. Demerits involve facing political wrath, potential professional harassment, and perceived insubordination.
3. Seek clarification and raise concerns internally: Merits include attempting resolution through dialogue, documenting concerns. Demerits include potential for DM to double down on pressure or perceive it as defiance.

(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
I would recommend Alok Sharma initially pursue Option 3, seeking a face-to-face meeting with the DM. He should respectfully present the factual ground realities with supporting data, explaining how manipulated figures would severely compromise the scheme's effectiveness, misguide future policy, and erode public trust in governance. He must articulate the long-term detrimental consequences for genuine beneficiaries and the administration's credibility.

If the DM insists on manipulation, Alok should politely but firmly express his inability to sign off on a false report, citing his oath of office and commitment to probity and integrity as per service rules. Simultaneously, he should meticulously document all instructions and interactions. If pressure escalates, he should be prepared to escalate the matter through appropriate official channels, such as reporting to the Chief Secretary or a higher vigilance authority, prioritizing the public interest over personal career safety compromised by unethical acts.
