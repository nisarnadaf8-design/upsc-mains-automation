Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Ms. Anjali Sharma, a dedicated and respected District Collector of Lalgiri, a tribal-majority district known for its rich biodiversity and cultural heritage, is overseeing local development projects. Her tenure has been marked by efforts to improve tribal welfare and ensure the implementation of forest rights.

Recently, the State Government issued a directive to expedite land acquisition for a critical industrial corridor project, citing urgent national economic interest and a strict six-month deadline. This project requires the diversion of significant ancestral forest land, impacting several tribal villages whose livelihoods are intrinsically linked to these forests. Ms. Sharma is now caught between the explicit orders from the state capital and the vehement protests from the tribal communities, supported by local NGOs, who highlight potential displacement, loss of traditional livelihoods, and violations of the Forest Rights Act and PESA provisions.

The Chief Secretary has personally called, emphasizing the project's strategic importance and hinting at adverse career implications for any delay. A powerful local politician, with vested interests in the industrial project, is actively campaigning for its rapid execution and is trying to discredit the tribal protests. Both action and inaction by Ms. Sharma have serious consequences, impacting not only her career but also the lives of thousands of tribal people and the state's economic development trajectory.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Ms. Anjali Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
