Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Aman Sharma, a young and diligent District Collector in the resource-rich tribal district of Janprabhat, has gained a reputation for his commitment to welfare schemes and community engagement. The district, home to several indigenous tribes, is marked by delicate ecological balances and deep ancestral ties of the tribal communities to their land and forests. His tenure so far has focused on ensuring development reaches the last mile while respecting tribal rights.

Recently, the State Government announced a large-scale industrial mining project in a tribal-majority block of Janprabhat, promising significant economic growth and employment. Aman has been issued an urgent directive by the Chief Secretary to expedite land acquisition and facilitate smooth project commencement, emphasizing its 'national importance' and potential revenue generation for the state. However, the proposed project site involves the displacement of several tribal villages and the acquisition of their sacred forest lands, where Gram Sabhas have not given their Free, Prior, and Informed Consent as mandated by the Forest Rights Act (FRA) and PESA Act.

Tribal elders, supported by local activists, have approached Aman with emotional appeals, highlighting their spiritual connection to the land and expressing profound fears of cultural annihilation and forced destitution if displaced. They cite previous instances of unfulfilled rehabilitation promises and irreversible environmental damage. Aman is caught between the explicit orders from the state government, which views any delay as insubordination, and his responsibility to protect the constitutional rights and welfare of the vulnerable tribal community.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Aman Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved from the state government and other stakeholders? (150 words)
