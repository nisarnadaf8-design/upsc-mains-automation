Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Manoj Kumar is serving as the District Collector of Shivpur, a district severely affected by unprecedented monsoon floods. As the nodal officer for disaster relief and rehabilitation, he is responsible for judiciously disbursing substantial government funds allocated for immediate aid to thousands of displaced and suffering citizens. The district is in a state of humanitarian crisis, with urgent needs for shelter, food, and medical supplies.

Amidst this crisis, Manoj faces immense pressure from the State Minister for Disaster Management, who instructs him to divert a significant portion of the relief funds to a 'special project' managed by an NGO closely associated with the Minister. The Minister argues this project is crucial for long-term rehabilitation, but it bypasses standard procurement protocols and appears designed to benefit certain contractors. He subtly warns Manoj of potential career repercussions if his directive is not followed. Simultaneously, local community leaders and ground-level volunteers are desperately appealing for immediate release of funds for urgent relief work.

Manoj is aware that diverting funds would severely compromise the immediate relief efforts, leaving countless flood victims without critical aid. However, defying a powerful political superior could jeopardize his career and future postings.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Manoj Kumar? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
