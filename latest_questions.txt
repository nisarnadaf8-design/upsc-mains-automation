Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Mr. Ankit Sharma, a relatively young and idealistic IAS officer from the 2023 batch, is currently posted as the Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM) in the rapidly urbanizing Mandpur District. Known for his commitment to public service and strict adherence to rules, he has recently been tasked with overseeing several crucial land acquisition processes for an ambitious infrastructure corridor project that promises to transform the district's connectivity.

During his review of the land records and compensation proposals, Ankit uncovers disturbing evidence of significant irregularities. Key agricultural lands, originally earmarked for acquisition, have been re-classified as barren or industrial plots at the behest of powerful local politicians, drastically reducing compensation for farmers while simultaneously increasing the valuation of adjacent parcels owned by a prominent private real estate developer, 'Zenith InfraCorp'. His immediate superior, the District Collector, subtly advises him to "expedite the process without getting bogged down in minor details," while a local MLA, a known associate of Zenith InfraCorp, has directly called Ankit, hinting at "future career opportunities" if he cooperates. He also faces immense pressure from the affected farmers who frequently visit his office, pleading for fair compensation and justice.

Ankit's decision will directly impact hundreds of marginal farmers and landless laborers who stand to lose their only source of livelihood, potentially triggering social unrest. It will also affect the integrity of the public administration, the credibility of the infrastructure project, and the financial interests of the private builder and connected political figures. Both taking action and remaining silent carry significant personal and professional risks for him.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Mr. Ankit Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
