Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

IAS Officer Kavita Sharma, a dedicated Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM) in the Revenue Department, was recently posted to the industrially developing district of Haritpur. Known for her integrity and proactive approach, she initiated a comprehensive drive to ensure compliance with labor and environmental standards across local factories. During one such routine inspection, her team identified a large manufacturing unit, 'Prosperity Textiles', for a follow-up.

During the unannounced follow-up inspection at 'Prosperity Textiles', Kavita's team uncovered irrefutable evidence of child labor, with several underage children working in hazardous conditions. Further investigation revealed the factory is beneficially owned by Mr. Rajesh Verma, a powerful sitting state minister and a key figure in the ruling party. Immediately, the District Collector advised a 'discreet and pragmatic handling' of the issue, citing political sensitivities, while an anonymous call from the Minister's office subtly hinted at significant career implications if the matter proceeded officially.

Kavita's decision will directly impact the exploited child laborers, the minister's political career and reputation, the factory's operations, and public trust in the administration. Both decisive action and cautious inaction carry severe consequences for her professional integrity, the rule of law, and the welfare of the most vulnerable.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Kavita Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
