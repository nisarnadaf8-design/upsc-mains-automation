Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Ms. Ananya Sharma, an honest and dedicated District Magistrate of Sundergarh district, Odisha, is spearheading the emergency response in May 2026. The district has recently been devastated by unprecedented monsoon floods, causing widespread displacement and destruction. The central government has allocated substantial emergency relief funds, and Ms. Sharma is personally overseeing their distribution to ensure swift and equitable aid reaches the affected population.

Just as the relief operations gain momentum, Ms. Sharma receives a direct order from a senior political leader to divert a significant portion of the allocated funds towards a road construction project in his constituency, citing 'long-term development' and 'political expediency' as reasons. Concurrently, her departmental secretary subtly hints that cooperation would ensure smoother career progression, while local community leaders from the most severely hit areas are clamoring for immediate and full aid.

The decision will profoundly affect thousands of flood victims awaiting essential supplies and shelter, as well as the credibility of the administration and public trust. Diverting funds could alleviate political pressure but leave vulnerable communities suffering, while refusing could lead to professional repercussions for Ms. Sharma and potential disruption of future relief efforts.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Ms. Ananya Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
