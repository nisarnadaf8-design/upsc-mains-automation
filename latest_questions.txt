Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Rohit Sharma, a newly appointed Superintendent of Police in the State Anti-Corruption Bureau (ACB) unit in Indirapuram, has built a reputation for his uncompromising integrity and sharp investigative skills. He is currently leading a high-profile investigation into a large-scale public procurement fraud involving several infrastructure projects in the state's Public Works Department (PWD).

During the course of the investigation, Rohit uncovers compelling evidence suggesting that the fraud is not limited to PWD but extends to a previous investigation conducted by his own ACB unit, involving collusion between a former senior officer of the ACB and some PWD officials. This discovery puts him in a profound ethical dilemma, as exposing this truth would not only tarnish the image of the ACB but also directly implicate his former superior, now a powerful Inspector General, and potentially embarrass the state government which prides itself on its anti-corruption initiatives.

His decision will significantly impact public trust in law enforcement, the credibility of the ACB, the careers of many officials, and the overall fight against corruption. Both pursuing the truth and attempting to suppress it carry severe implications for justice and ethical governance.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Rohit Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
