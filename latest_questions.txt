Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Aparna Singh, a dynamic young District Collector of Mandla district, Madhya Pradesh, has been lauded for her pro-people approach. Her current challenge involves a state-backed mining project for bauxite extraction in the dense forest region, impacting several Gond tribal villages. The project promises significant revenue and jobs but threatens the ancestral lands and traditional livelihoods of the tribal communities.

The State Government has issued clear directives to fast-track environmental clearances and land acquisition, citing national interest and economic growth. However, local tribal leaders, supported by environmental activists, are vehemently opposing it, highlighting potential ecological devastation, displacement, and violation of the Forest Rights Act (FRA) and PESA. Aparna is under immense pressure from the Chief Secretary to demonstrate 'administrative efficiency' and also faces passionate appeals from the tribal community for protecting their jal, jangal, zameen.

The decision will directly impact thousands of tribal families, the district's ecological balance, the state's revenue goals, and potentially set a precedent for future development projects. Both swift compliance and empathetic resistance carry significant socio-economic, environmental, and political consequences for Aparna's career and the community's future.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Aparna Singh? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
