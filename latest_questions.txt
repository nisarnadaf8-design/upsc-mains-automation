Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Aakash Sharma, a diligent District Environmental Officer in the State Pollution Control Board, is posted in Rudrapur, a rapidly industrializing district. His role involves ensuring industries comply with environmental regulations and issuing compliance reports. Recently, his team conducted an inspection of 'Greenfield Petrochemicals Ltd.', a major employer and influential company in the region.

The inspection report detailed significant violations related to untreated industrial discharge into a local river and improper hazardous waste disposal. However, before Aakash could initiate action, he received a call from his Principal Secretary, subtly hinting at "exercising discretion" due to the company's economic contribution. A local political leader also directly urged him to re-evaluate the findings, suggesting strict action could jeopardize the region's industrial climate and Aakash's career, while the company CEO offered to "contribute generously" to local development if the matter was resolved amicably.

Aakash faces an acute ethical dilemma. Approving the report means potential confrontation with his superiors and powerful political figures, risking his career and institutional pressure. Overlooking the violations would endanger the health of local communities dependent on the river, degrade the environment, and compromise the integrity of his office and the regulatory body.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Aakash Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
