Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Ms. Anjali Sharma, District Project Director in the Rural Development Agency of a drought-prone district, is overseeing a major irrigation infrastructure project crucial for regional water security and agricultural productivity. The project has recently faced some local protests regarding land acquisition compensation, although the process was meticulously followed as per government guidelines.

A local activist and RTI applicant has filed a request demanding detailed project files, including strategic cost estimates, internal feasibility reports, and proposed contractor evaluation metrics. Ms. Sharma understands the public's right to information but is also aware that releasing specific, highly sensitive financial and strategic details could prematurely escalate land compensation demands, jeopardize ongoing negotiations, or even create security risks for potential contractors by revealing their detailed profiles. Her superior, the District Magistrate, has advised caution, citing the paramount 'public interest' in ensuring the project's timely and cost-effective completion. Simultaneously, a prominent local political leader has warned against revealing data that could be exploited by vested interests, while the RTI applicant threatens widespread public agitation if the information is withheld.

Ms. Sharma recognizes that her decision will have far-reaching implications. Releasing the sensitive information could lead to significant project cost overruns, legal entanglements, and unwarranted delays, ultimately harming the very communities the project intends to serve. Conversely, withholding the information entirely could be perceived as a lack of transparency, erode public trust, and ignite further social unrest, potentially stalling the project altogether.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Ms. Anjali Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
