Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Divya Sharma is the District Magistrate of Harinagar, a rapidly developing district in Uttar Pradesh, known for its agricultural produce and emerging industrial zones. She is known for her integrity and efficient handling of land acquisition disputes, a frequent occurrence in the district due to infrastructure projects. Her father, Mr. Om Prakash Sharma, a respected retired government school teacher, recently decided to invest his life savings in a small plot of land for a new dairy farm business with a local entrepreneur.

A few weeks later, a major railway corridor project, sanctioned at the national level, passes through Harinagar, and Divya's office is tasked with overseeing the land acquisition process, including dispute resolution. To her shock, she discovers that the land her father recently purchased falls directly within the proposed railway alignment and is now subject to compulsory acquisition. Her father, unaware of the project details, stands to lose significant investment if the land is acquired at the current market rate, which is considerably lower than its potential future value. Local political leaders, aware of the situation, subtly pressure Divya to "expedite" the acquisition process for other plots but show "flexibility" for her father's plot, hinting at a more favorable compensation package or even an alteration in the alignment, citing "public interest adjustments." Meanwhile, her family expects her to protect their interests, and the entrepreneur is threatening legal action if the investment is lost.

Her decision will directly impact her father, the entrepreneur, other landowners affected by the project, the credibility of the district administration, and the timely completion of a crucial national infrastructure project. Any perception of bias could lead to public protests and accusations of corruption, while strict adherence to rules might cause personal hardship and familial discord.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Divya Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
