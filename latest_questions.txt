Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Mr. Alok Kumar, District Collector of the predominantly tribal Surajpur District, has been instrumental in implementing several welfare schemes. The district is earmarked for a significant industrial corridor project, a flagship initiative of the state government, promising economic growth and employment for the region.

The detailed Environmental and Social Impact Assessment (ESIA) report, though officially cleared, has serious flaws. It underestimates the displacement of several indigenous tribal hamlets and the irreversible damage to their sacred groves and traditional livelihoods, which rely heavily on forest produce. The Chief Secretary has conveyed the state's firm commitment to the project, emphasizing its strategic importance and instructing Alok to fast-track land acquisition. Simultaneously, a powerful local tribal council leader has warned of mass protests and even self-immolation if their ancestral lands are forcibly taken.

Stakeholders include the tribal communities facing displacement, the state government pushing for industrial development, potential investors, and the environmental integrity of the region. Alok's decision will have profound implications for the human rights of the tribals, the state's development agenda, and his own career trajectory and reputation for integrity.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Mr. Alok Kumar? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
