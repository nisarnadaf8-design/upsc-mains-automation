Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Rakesh Sharma, a newly appointed District Magistrate in Ramgarh, a district with significant tribal populations and rich biodiversity, is tasked with overseeing a critical national highway expansion project. The project is vital for regional connectivity and economic growth, having been a long-standing promise by the central government and a key development initiative.

A substantial segment of the proposed highway route cuts through ancestral lands, sacred groves, and a protected forest area traditionally used by the indigenous communities for sustenance and cultural practices. Despite initial consultations and environmental impact assessments, local tribal groups are now fiercely resisting land acquisition, citing violations of their forest rights and potential destruction of their unique cultural heritage. The Chief Secretary is pressing Rakesh to expedite land acquisition and ensure the project remains on schedule, while an influential local MLA is publicly championing the tribal cause, warning of widespread unrest if their demands are not met.

The decision impacts thousands of tribal families facing displacement, the regional economy dependent on the highway, and the credibility of the administration in upholding both developmental goals and community rights. Both proceeding forcefully with acquisition and stalling the project indefinitely carry significant social, economic, environmental, and political ramifications.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Rakesh Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
