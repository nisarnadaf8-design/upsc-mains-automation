Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Aarti Sharma, a newly appointed Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM) in the rapidly urbanizing district of Bharatpur, is a sincere and idealistic IAS officer. Her responsibilities include overseeing land acquisition processes for public infrastructure projects and maintaining land records. She is committed to ensuring transparency and fairness in her administration.

During her review of a large-scale land acquisition for a proposed industrial corridor, Aarti uncovers significant discrepancies. She finds evidence suggesting that compensation packages for several plots have been deliberately undervalued, while certain land parcels belonging to politically connected private builders were acquired at exorbitant rates, far exceeding market value. Her immediate superior, the District Collector, subtly advises her to expedite the process without further scrutiny, citing 'development urgency,' while a prominent local political leader directly contacts her to ensure 'smooth passage' of the files.

The affected farmers, who stand to lose their ancestral land with inadequate compensation, are largely unaware of the extent of the undervaluation. Failure to act would perpetuate injustice and institutionalize corruption, while initiating an inquiry could jeopardize Aarti's career, invite political backlash, and potentially delay the crucial industrial project, affecting the district's development prospects.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Aarti Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
