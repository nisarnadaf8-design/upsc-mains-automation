Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Meera Sharma, a young and dedicated Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM) in the flood-ravaged district of Sagar, is leading a critical team coordinating relief and rehabilitation efforts. The district has been severely hit by unprecedented rains, displacing thousands and causing extensive damage to infrastructure. She has been working relentlessly for weeks, often alongside her superior, the District Collector (DC), Mr. Alok Kumar, whom she deeply respects as a mentor.

During an internal audit of procurement for emergency shelters and food supplies, Meera uncovers compelling evidence of large-scale corruption. Her investigation reveals that Mr. Kumar is systematically diverting funds meant for victims, approving grossly inflated bills for substandard materials, and favouring a specific network of contractors. The dilemma is stark: Mr. Kumar is not only her direct superior and a politically influential figure but also a personal mentor, and exposing him could severely disrupt ongoing relief operations, jeopardize her career, and potentially put her in personal danger.

The primary stakeholders are the vulnerable flood victims who are being denied essential aid, the honest officers working diligently, the reputation of the civil service, and Meera's own integrity and future. Inaction would perpetuate suffering and systemic corruption, while action carries significant personal and institutional risks, yet upholds ethical governance.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Meera Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
