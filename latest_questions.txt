Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Rahul Sharma, a newly posted District Magistrate in Sarvodaya, a rapidly developing district, is known for his unblemished integrity and meticulous adherence to administrative rules. His district is currently overseeing several significant infrastructure projects, including a major national highway expansion and related urban development. Rahul is in the midst of reviewing land acquisition files for a proposed industrial park, which requires stringent environmental clearances and public consultations.

A powerful state minister, Mr. Rakesh Verma, directly approaches Rahul, requesting him to fast-track the land acquisition for a private real estate project owned by his close associate, Mr. Khanna. This project involves a protected wetland area not designated for commercial development and would necessitate bending several environmental regulations, bypassing the mandatory public hearing process, and overlooking concerns raised by local farmer groups. Rahul's immediate superior, the Divisional Commissioner, subtly advises him to be "pragmatic" and consider the "political sensitivities" involved, while local community leaders have already started protesting against any such move. Rahul’s family, concerned for his career and safety, urges caution.

The officer's decision will directly impact hundreds of marginal farmers and tribal communities who stand to lose their land and traditional livelihoods. Furthermore, the ecological balance of the region will be severely disturbed, and the integrity of the land acquisition process compromised. Both compliance and refusal carry significant consequences for Rahul, the administration, and the public trust.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Rahul Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
