Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Rahul Sharma is a diligent District Magistrate in Bharatpur District, known for his integrity and efficiency. His current focus is the successful completion and allocation of units under a flagship public housing scheme for Economically Weaker Sections (EWS), a project he has managed transparently from its inception. The final list of verified beneficiaries is ready for approval.

Suddenly, a powerful ruling party MLA from a neighbouring constituency, Mr. Singh, approaches Rahul with an unofficial list, demanding that several completed housing units be reallocated to his political supporters, many of whom clearly do not meet the strict eligibility criteria. Mr. Singh suggests that non-compliance could lead to "unnecessary scrutiny" of Rahul's work or a "challenging transfer." Rahul's immediate superior, the Divisional Commissioner, later advises him to "find an amicable solution" to avoid political friction, implying flexibility is expected.

If Rahul were to comply, numerous deserving EWS families would be deprived of their legitimate homes and the integrity of a vital social welfare program would be compromised. If he refuses, he risks facing severe political repercussions and potential career instability, while also navigating the implicit pressure from his superior.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Rahul Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
