Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Smt. Priya Sharma, a diligent Deputy Commissioner in District Sagar, was overseeing several large-scale infrastructure projects under the Department of Rural Development. During her review of a major government-funded rural road project, she meticulously uncovered substantial financial irregularities, involving inflated bills for raw materials and substandard construction practices. She documented all evidence thoroughly and submitted a detailed report directly to the Chief Secretary's office.

Immediately after her report, Smt. Sharma found herself subjected to covert threats, anonymous complaints about her own conduct circulated within the department, and an unofficial departmental inquiry initiated by a politically influential senior officer, who was reportedly implicated in the fraud. Furthermore, her family began facing indirect targeting through false allegations against a small business they operated, creating immense personal pressure and distress.

Her actions have brought the integrity of the entire project and the reputation of the department into question. The local public, aware of the substandard road quality, is keenly watching the outcome, while inaction would perpetuate corruption and undermine public trust.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Smt. Priya Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
