Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Mr. Alok Verma, an Under Secretary in the Strategic Planning Division of the Ministry of Defence, New Delhi, is responsible for processing sensitive information requests. His department recently oversaw the planning and initial execution of a critical dual-use infrastructure project – a major highway connecting strategic border regions.

He has received an RTI application from a public interest researcher seeking granular details regarding the project's budget allocation, specific land acquisition processes, and the full environmental impact assessment. While his Joint Secretary strongly advises rejecting the application citing national security under Section 8(1)(a) of the RTI Act, local community organizations have publicly expressed concerns about transparency, potential environmental damage, and fair compensation for displaced families. There are also subtle signals from the Defence Minister's office about the project's classified nature and its geopolitical implications.

Alok's decision will directly impact the RTI applicant, the affected local communities, the Ministry's commitment to transparency, and potentially national security interests. Both providing the information and withholding it carry significant ethical and practical consequences.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Alok Verma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
