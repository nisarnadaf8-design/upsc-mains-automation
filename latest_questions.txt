Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Riya Sharma, a young and committed District Development Officer in Palamau district, Jharkhand, is overseeing the implementation of a flagship central government welfare scheme, 'Gram Uday Yojana', aimed at improving rural livelihoods through skill development and micro-enterprise support. The scheme is in its crucial second year, and Riya is responsible for compiling the annual impact report based on ground-level data collection from various blocks.

As the deadline for the report approaches, Riya's immediate superior, the District Collector, along with a prominent local MLA, strongly suggests that the beneficiary numbers and impact metrics be 'adjusted' upwards to show greater success, citing pressure from state headquarters and the upcoming elections. They imply that failure to meet inflated targets could jeopardise future funding for the district and Riya's own career progression, while highlighting the importance of 'teamwork' and 'administrative flexibility'.

Riya knows that manipulating the data would present a misleading picture, depriving genuine beneficiaries of necessary support and misallocating public funds. Conversely, reporting the accurate, less impressive data could lead to political backlash, potential transfer to a less desirable posting, and strain her professional relationships within the district administration, potentially affecting other ongoing projects. The real impact on the ground, the public trust in governance, and the efficacy of welfare policies are at stake.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Riya Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
