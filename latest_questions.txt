Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Mr. Alok Kumar, a diligent District Collector in the economically backward district of Ramanagar, is overseeing a critical land acquisition project for a major industrial corridor aimed at generating local employment. The project involves acquiring significant tracts of agricultural land from several villages, leading to intense public scrutiny and protests regarding fair compensation and rehabilitation packages.

A sudden complication arises when Mr. Kumar discovers that a significant portion of the land earmarked for acquisition is owned by a real estate firm, 'Shakti Properties', in which his elder brother holds a substantial stake. His brother directly approaches him, subtly suggesting the possibility of a revised land valuation or a favourable interpretation of rehabilitation policies for his firm, citing potential financial ruin for the family business.

The Chief Secretary has set a strict deadline for the project's completion, local political leaders are vocal, some supporting the displaced farmers while others align with industrial interests. The decision will profoundly impact hundreds of farming families, the financial viability of his brother's firm, and the public's trust in the impartiality of the administration and Mr. Kumar's personal integrity.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Mr. Alok Kumar? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend to Mr. Kumar and why? How would he handle the various pressures involved? (150 words)
