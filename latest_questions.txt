Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Rohan Sharma, a bright and idealistic IAS officer, recently posted as Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM) in the Revenue Department of a rapidly urbanizing district in a southern Indian state, is overseeing several key projects, including a major land acquisition for an ambitious smart city development.

He discovers that a significant portion of the acquired agricultural land, initially earmarked for public infrastructure, is being quietly re-allotted to a consortium of private builders at highly concessional rates, bypassing transparent bidding processes. This appears driven by a powerful state minister and supported by his District Collector, who advises him to "expedite approvals without asking too many questions". The local farmers' union has begun protesting, alleging a scam.

The key stakeholders are the marginalized farmers, the private builders, the state exchequer, the public's trust in governance, and Rohan's professional integrity. Both compliance and resistance carry severe personal and public consequences, impacting justice, transparency, and administrative ethics.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Rohan Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
