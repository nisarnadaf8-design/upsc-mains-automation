Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Ramesh Kumar, a conscientious District Magistrate in the rural district of Mandla, Madhya Pradesh, is known for his integrity and commitment to fair administration. His current responsibility includes overseeing the distribution of disaster relief funds and the verification of beneficiary certificates for agricultural subsidies following a recent severe drought.

Just before a crucial state election, the local Member of Parliament and a senior leader of the ruling political party, Mr. Verma, approaches Ramesh. Mr. Verma insists that Ramesh's office expedite the issuance of several hundred 'false calamity loss certificates' to ineligible party loyalists, ensuring their votes. Simultaneously, Ramesh's immediate superior, the Divisional Commissioner, subtly hints at cooperation, mentioning 'career implications' if the party's request is not met. Ramesh is also aware that denying these influential figures could lead to his transfer to an inconsequential posting, impacting his family's stability and his ability to serve the genuinely needy.

Ramesh's decision will directly affect the integrity of public funds, the trust of genuine drought victims, and the fairness of the electoral process. Both compliance and defiance carry significant personal, professional, and systemic consequences.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Ramesh Kumar? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
