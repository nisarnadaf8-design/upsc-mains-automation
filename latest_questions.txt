Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Ms. Riya Sharma, a young and dedicated IAS officer, is serving as the District Collector of Kamalnagar, a district rich in forest resources and predominantly inhabited by indigenous tribal communities in a central Indian state. Her tenure has been marked by efforts to implement welfare schemes and resolve land-related issues for the vulnerable tribal population, earning her trust among them.

Recently, the State Government has fast-tracked a large-scale industrial project, promising significant investment and employment, which requires the immediate acquisition of several hundred acres of land. This land, crucial for the project, includes sacred groves and agricultural fields belonging to a particularly vulnerable tribal group, traditionally protected under the Forest Rights Act. Riya has received explicit orders from the Chief Secretary to ensure swift land acquisition, bypassing some standard consultative procedures, citing urgent economic development. However, tribal representatives have approached her with petitions detailing the potential displacement, loss of livelihood, and irreversible cultural damage without proper rehabilitation.

Her decision will directly impact thousands of tribal families, the state's economic development trajectory, and her career prospects. Both strict adherence to government orders and prioritising tribal welfare carry serious repercussions, ranging from legal challenges and widespread public protests to potential disciplinary action for insubordination and hampering state development.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Riya Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
