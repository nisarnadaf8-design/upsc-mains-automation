Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Divya Sharma, a newly appointed Sub-Divisional Magistrate in the culturally sensitive Rampur sub-division of District Avantika, is facing her first major challenge. A minor altercation during a local festival has quickly flared into widespread communal tension, with small-scale skirmishes and incendiary social media posts creating a volatile atmosphere in several localities. The District Collector has entrusted her with restoring peace and order.

As Divya attempts to gather objective ground reports and implement impartial measures, she receives a direct call from the powerful local MLA, Mr. Rakesh Verma. He insists that she must immediately take strict action against a particular community, accusing them solely of instigating the violence, and suggests detaining specific individuals without due process. Mr. Verma explicitly warns her that non-compliance would lead to her swift transfer and career difficulties, asserting that "public sentiment demands decisive action." Meanwhile, local intelligence indicates that the conflict is more nuanced, involving provocations from multiple sides.

Divya's decision will directly impact the lives and safety of thousands of citizens, the credibility of the district administration, and the future communal harmony of Rampur. Failing to act impartially could escalate the conflict into widespread violence and undermine the rule of law, while upholding her ethical duty risks her professional standing and relationship with political masters.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Divya Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
