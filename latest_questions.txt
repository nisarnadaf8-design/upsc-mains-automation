========================================

Q2. (10 Marks | 150 words)

Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Ms. Ananya Sharma, a diligent Deputy Secretary in the Ministry of Finance, uncovered a significant financial fraud involving fraudulent claims and embezzlement of public funds worth crores in a major infrastructure project. Following due process, she meticulously documented the irregularities and submitted a detailed report to the Vigilance Commissioner and her Joint Secretary.

Shortly after her report, Ms. Sharma began experiencing subtle but clear retaliation. Her work was being unduly scrutinized, important files were withheld, and her transfer to a remote, inconsequential post was being actively considered by her Joint Secretary, who appeared to be acting under pressure from a powerful political figure implicated in the fraud. Her family is deeply worried about her career and safety, urging her to retract or dilute her findings to avoid further persecution.

Ms. Sharma's decision will directly impact the integrity of public service, the financial health of the state, the morale of honest officers, and the security and peace of mind of her family. Both pursuing the truth and succumbing to pressure carry severe, long-term consequences for all involved.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Ms. Ananya Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
