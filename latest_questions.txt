Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Ms. Ananya Sharma is a Joint Secretary in the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change in New Delhi. Her department is responsible for processing environmental clearances for large infrastructure projects. Recently, a major multi-state river interlinking project, involving significant land acquisition in tribal-dominated forest areas and critical wildlife habitats, received provisional environmental clearance. This project is deemed strategically important for water security and regional development.

A prominent environmental activist and RTI applicant, Mr. Rajeev Mehta, has filed a comprehensive RTI request seeking detailed reports, internal committee deliberations, expert opinions, and any dissenting notes related to the Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) of this particular project. Ms. Sharma's superior, the Secretary, has informally advised her to withhold certain 'sensitive' information, citing national strategic interests, potential public unrest, and the risk of derailing crucial foreign investment tied to the project. The concerned state governments are also exerting pressure through informal channels for quick commencement, fearing delays could cause political backlash.

Ms. Sharma's decision will directly impact the project's progress, the rights and livelihoods of thousands of tribal communities, the Ministry's credibility regarding transparency, and public trust in environmental governance. Both releasing all information and withholding it completely carry significant consequences.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Ms. Ananya Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
