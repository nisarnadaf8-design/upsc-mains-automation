Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Aarav Sharma, a dedicated young Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM) in the Revenue Department, has recently been posted to a sub-division in District Bhagalpur, Bihar. His office is critical for issuing various public certificates, including domicile, income, and caste certificates, which are essential for electoral eligibility, welfare scheme access, and educational opportunities in the run-up to imminent local body elections.

Aarav finds himself under intense pressure from the incumbent ruling party's local MLA, who is also a distant relative. The MLA explicitly demands the expedited issuance of a large number of domicile and income certificates to individuals who appear to lack genuine eligibility, explicitly stating these are crucial for "party loyalists" to secure voting rights and welfare benefits. The MLA subtly reminds Aarav of the implications for his career progression and potential transfers, emphasizing the need for "administrative flexibility" and "political accommodation." Simultaneously, Aarav's immediate superior, the District Collector, in a veiled conversation, advises him to "maintain cordial relations with local political leadership for smooth governance," without giving a direct order to comply. Aarav is also aware that some individuals seeking these certificates, though lacking documentation, are genuinely marginalized and may have been failed by the system previously.

Aarav's decision will directly impact the fairness and integrity of the electoral process, the equitable distribution of public resources, and the public's perception of administrative impartiality. Issuing false certificates would undermine the rule of law and potentially incite social unrest, while a refusal could lead to significant professional and political repercussions, including social isolation and adverse career actions.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Aarav Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved from both the political leader and your superior? (150 words)
