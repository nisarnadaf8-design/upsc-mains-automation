Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Sanjeev Kumar is the District Collector of Bhairavpur District, a semi-hilly region known for its unique biodiversity and indigenous communities. He is currently tasked with overseeing a high-priority national infrastructure project: the construction of a critical high-speed rail tunnel aimed at linking remote areas to the national grid and stimulating economic growth. The project has an aggressive timeline and significant national strategic importance.

A major challenge has emerged as the proposed tunnel route passes directly beneath a sacred grove, known as "Devbhoomi," which is revered as ancestral land by the indigenous 'Vanvasi' community of Pathargarh village. This community asserts that the grove is central to their spiritual identity and traditional way of life, and its disturbance would be an act of sacrilege and ecological destruction. They present valid historical claims and environmental impact concerns. However, any significant rerouting of the project would incur massive cost overruns, delay the project significantly, and potentially jeopardize its national importance and funding. Sanjeev is facing immense pressure from his superiors regarding project deadlines, from the local political leadership (the MLA, who initially supported the project but is now swayed by community sentiments), and from the project developers who threaten legal action for any delays.

The Vanvasi community faces potential displacement, loss of cultural heritage, and environmental degradation, leading to a deep erosion of trust in the administration. Conversely, delaying or altering the project would result in substantial financial losses, hinder national development goals, and invite political backlash, affecting the public perception of governance efficacy.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Sanjeev Kumar? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
