Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words)
Rohan Sharma, a young and committed District Collector, is posted in the remote, forest-rich district of Dantewada, Chhattisgarh. His primary challenge is overseeing the implementation of a strategically important iron ore mining project, critical for national infrastructure development, which involves significant land acquisition and displacement of several indigenous tribal villages adhering to traditional forest-based livelihoods.

The project, while mandated by the central government and championed by senior bureaucrats and state political leadership for its economic benefits, faces fierce resistance from the local tribal communities. They invoke their rights under the PESA Act and Forest Rights Act, expressing deep concerns over loss of ancestral land, cultural identity, environmental degradation, and inadequate rehabilitation packages. Rohan finds himself caught between the pressure from his Chief Secretary to expedite clearances and ensure smooth project execution, and the legitimate grievances and planned protests from the tribal groups, supported by various civil society organizations.

His decision will profoundly impact the lives and livelihoods of thousands of tribal families, the financial viability of a national strategic project, and the socio-environmental fabric of the region. Both swift project approval and outright rejection carry significant economic, social, and political ramifications, potentially leading to social unrest, legal challenges, or substantial delays in a critical national undertaking.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Rohan Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
