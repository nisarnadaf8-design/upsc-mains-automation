Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Ms. Anjali Sharma, a newly appointed District Magistrate of Rohanpur District, Maharashtra, is known for her diligence and commitment to social welfare programs. During a routine inspection of industrial units in an underdeveloped zone, her team discovers a mid-sized textile factory operating without proper clearances and, more alarmingly, employing several underage children in hazardous conditions.

Further investigation reveals that the factory is beneficially owned by a powerful sitting Member of Parliament (MP) from the ruling party, a senior politician with significant influence at state and national levels. Her immediate superior, the Divisional Commissioner, subtly advises caution, hinting at the political sensitivities involved and potential career repercussions for Ms. Sharma. The factory owner, through intermediaries, offers a substantial 'settlement' to avoid official action and media attention.

The children employed, their impoverished families who depend on the meager income, the powerful factory owner (MP), the district administration's credibility, the general public's faith in the rule of law, and Ms. Sharma's own career and integrity are all at stake. Taking immediate, decisive action could lead to political backlash and personal victimisation, while inaction would perpetuate a grave injustice and undermine the fundamental principles of governance.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case.
(b) What are the options available to Ms. Anjali Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits.
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved to ensure justice?
