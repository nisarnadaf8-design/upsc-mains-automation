Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Pranjal Sharma, a dedicated Joint Secretary in the Ministry of Commerce and Industry, based in New Delhi, has served with an exemplary record for twenty years, known for his integrity and efficiency. His department is responsible for approving large industrial projects and managing international trade agreements.

Recently, Pranjal uncovered substantial evidence of a sophisticated financial fraud scheme involving a prominent industrialist and a few high-ranking officials within his own ministry, potentially compromising a major international trade deal. He diligently compiled a detailed report and, upholding his ethical duty, submitted it to the appropriate vigilance authorities and his immediate superior, becoming an internal whistleblower. Following his report, Pranjal has been subjected to immense pressure: his career progression has been stalled, his family has received anonymous threats, and he faces subtle ostracism from colleagues who are either complicit or fearful. His superior, a long-time acquaintance, privately advises him to retract his allegations, suggesting it's "better for everyone involved" and crucial for his own and his family's security.

Pranjal's decision will not only affect his personal safety and career but also the morale of honest officers, the integrity of the Ministry, the nation's economic interests, and public trust in governance. Both pursuing the truth and backing down carry severe personal, professional, and systemic consequences.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Pranjal Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
