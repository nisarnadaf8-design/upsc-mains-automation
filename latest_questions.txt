Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Anjali Sharma, a young IAS officer serving as the Sub-Divisional Magistrate in the rapidly urbanizing district of Krishnapuram, is overseeing a crucial land acquisition process for a major highway expansion project under a public-private partnership. The project involves a prominent real estate conglomerate.

During her diligent review of land records and compensation assessments, Anjali uncovers glaring irregularities, including undervaluation of prime agricultural land, discrepancies in ownership documents favoring speculators, and instances where vulnerable farmers appear coerced into signing consent forms for inadequate compensation. Her initial attempts to seek clarification are met with passive resistance from junior revenue staff. Subsequently, the District Collector, her immediate superior, advises her to "expedite the process without getting bogged down in minor details," hinting at significant political pressure from a powerful state minister closely associated with the private builder. Anjali faces a profound ethical conflict between her duty to ensure justice and the explicit pressure to conform, knowing that raising formal objections could severely jeopardize her burgeoning career.

The primary stakeholders in this complex situation include the marginalized farmers facing displacement, the private developer seeking rapid project completion, local politicians with vested interests, the District Collector, and the general public whose trust in the fairness of governance is at stake. Both swift approval and courageous intervention carry significant consequences for Anjali and the community.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Anjali Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
