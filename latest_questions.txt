Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Rohan Sharma is the District Planning Officer in the Department of Rural Development in Mandi District, Himachal Pradesh. He is currently overseeing the final compilation of an impact assessment report for the flagship ‘Gramin Uday’ welfare scheme, which is crucial for securing the next tranche of central government funding for rural development projects. The raw data collected from the ground reveals mixed results, with some negative trends regarding beneficiary outreach and the actual impact on livelihoods in remote villages.

His superior, the District Collector, has subtly suggested "optimizing" the data to present a more "favorable" report, emphasizing the need to project success to secure future funds and maintain the district's reputation. Simultaneously, the local Member of Legislative Assembly (MLA) has also hinted at the political importance of showcasing the scheme's achievements, given the upcoming state elections. Rohan's own career progression is closely tied to the perceived success of such schemes, yet he is deeply aware that manipulating the data would mask genuine issues faced by the rural poor.

The beneficiaries of the Gramin Uday scheme are genuinely struggling with implementation gaps and poor service delivery, which would be completely overlooked if the report is manipulated. Both submitting the factual report and yielding to pressure have significant consequences for the public, government credibility, and Rohan’s personal and professional life.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Rohan Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
