Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Ritesh Kumar serves as the District Education Officer in a politically sensitive district, Bikaner, within a state gearing up for assembly elections. A new centrally sponsored scheme, "Vidya Setu Yojana," aims to provide substantial scholarships to meritorious students from economically weaker sections, with strict eligibility criteria for parental income and academic performance. The scheme's implementation is crucial for the district's development indicators.

Just weeks before the scholarship application deadline, Ritesh receives direct instructions from the powerful local MLA, who also holds a cabinet portfolio, to relax the eligibility norms and issue false income certificates to hundreds of students from specific constituencies, ensuring they qualify for the scheme. The MLA explicitly states this is vital for securing votes for the ruling party, hinting at serious career repercussions for Ritesh if he fails to comply. Simultaneously, a local NGO representative approaches Ritesh, alleging widespread corruption in the certificate issuance process and threatening public exposure.

Ritesh, the MLA, deserving students, undeserving beneficiaries, the Education Department, public trust in governance, and the integrity of the scholarship scheme itself are the key stakeholders. Compliance would compromise fairness and resource allocation, while defiance risks Ritesh's career and potentially public order.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Ritesh Kumar? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
