Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Ms. Ananya Sharma is the District Collector of Bastar district in Chhattisgarh, a region known for its rich tribal heritage and dense forest cover. A major state-approved mining project, crucial for state revenue and infrastructure development, requires the acquisition of a significant tract of forested land predominantly inhabited by the Gond tribal community, who depend on these forests for their livelihood and cultural practices. Ms. Sharma, relatively new to the district, is admired for her dedication and understanding of local issues.

The tribal community, fearing displacement and environmental damage, has launched widespread protests against the project, despite offers of compensation. Ms. Sharma receives direct orders from the Chief Secretary to expedite the land acquisition process, citing the project's strategic importance and national interest. Simultaneously, a prominent tribal rights activist, supported by a powerful local political leader, mobilizes intense public resistance, demanding Ms. Sharma halt proceedings and appealing to her sense of justice and empathy for the marginalized tribal population.

Her decision will directly impact thousands of tribal families, their ancestral lands, and the region's delicate ecosystem. Delaying the project risks jeopardizing critical state development goals, incurring severe political disapproval, and potential legal ramifications, while proceeding risks widespread social unrest, displacement, and a permanent erosion of trust between the administration and the tribal community.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Ms. Ananya Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
