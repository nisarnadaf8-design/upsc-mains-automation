========================================

Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Ms. Priya Sharma, a young and upright IAS officer, serves as the District Magistrate of the flood-prone district of Malgaon. In July 2026, severe monsoon floods have devastated the region, rendering thousands homeless and critically short of food, shelter, and medical supplies. Ms. Sharma is directly responsible for the immediate allocation and transparent disbursement of emergency relief funds sanctioned by the central government, amounting to ₹50 crores.

Amidst the chaos and urgent need, Ms. Sharma receives direct instructions from a senior political leader, the State Minister for Disaster Management, to divert 10% of the allocated funds towards rebuilding a temple in his constituency that suffered structural damage, claiming it is essential for public morale and community cohesion. Simultaneously, her immediate superior, the Divisional Commissioner, subtly advises her to prioritize specific contractors for supply tenders, hinting at 'expediting processes' given the emergency, which Ms. Sharma knows are connected to local influential individuals with a history of questionable practices. The affected populace, meanwhile, is desperate and growing increasingly restless, anticipating immediate and equitable aid.

Priya's decision will directly impact the lives and survival of thousands of flood victims dependent on timely and honest aid. Any diversion or delay risks exacerbating the humanitarian crisis, eroding public trust in the administration, and compromising her administrative integrity and career, while defying superiors could lead to punitive transfers or harassment.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Ms. Priya Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
