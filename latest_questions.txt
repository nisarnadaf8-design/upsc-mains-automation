Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Superintendent of Police, Ms. Ananya Sharma, posted in the industrially growing district of Rampur, [State], has been lauded for her integrity and proactive approach to law enforcement. Her current assignment involves overseeing a major public safety project to install smart surveillance systems across the district, funded significantly by the state government, aiming to curb rising crime rates.

During a routine audit of project expenditures, Ms. Sharma uncovers discrepancies suggesting large-scale embezzlement involving inflated contracts and non-existent procurements. The preliminary findings point towards the involvement of several senior police officials within her own department, including a close aide of the Inspector General. A local political leader, whose associates secured some of the questionable contracts, has subtly warned her against 'disrupting the peace' if she pursues the matter further.

Her decision to expose the scam could lead to a major shake-up within the police hierarchy, loss of public trust if concealed, and potential threats to her career and personal safety. Conversely, inaction would compromise her integrity, perpetuate corruption, and deprive citizens of essential safety infrastructure. The public, the police force, and the state's reputation are all at stake.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Ms. Ananya Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
