Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Rakesh Kumar, an Under Secretary in the Department of Defence Procurement, Ministry of Defence, New Delhi, is responsible for processing sensitive government contracts. His department recently concluded negotiations for a critical defense system, a process involving national security considerations and substantial public funds.

An RTI applicant has sought detailed records, including internal deliberations and commercial terms, related to this procurement. While Rakesh believes in transparency, a confidential circular from his Joint Secretary explicitly warns against disclosing information that could compromise national security or ongoing diplomatic relations. Simultaneously, the Minister's office has indirectly communicated the political sensitivity of the matter, fearing public backlash or international implications if certain details are prematurely revealed.

His decision will impact the RTI applicant's right to information, national security, the integrity of ongoing diplomatic negotiations, the public's trust in government, and his own career progression. Both full disclosure and complete denial carry significant and potentially damaging consequences.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Rakesh Kumar? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
