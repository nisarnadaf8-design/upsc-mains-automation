Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Ms. Ananya Sharma is the District Collector of Sundergarh District, Odisha, a region known for its rich biodiversity and significant tribal population. The central government has identified Sundergarh as a strategic location for establishing a National Green Hydrogen Hub, a project crucial for India's energy security and transition to renewable sources. The project requires the acquisition of a large tract of land, which includes 'Jalgaon', a Scheduled Tribe village whose residents hold ancestral land rights protected under the PESA Act and consider certain forest patches as sacred groves vital to their cultural identity.

The tribal community of Jalgaon has vehemently resisted displacement, citing threats to their livelihoods, cultural heritage, and the fragile ecosystem. Local political leaders, supported by several environmental and tribal rights NGOs, have mobilized public opinion against the project. Simultaneously, Ms. Sharma is facing intense pressure from the Chief Secretary and the Union Minister for Energy to expedite the land acquisition process, who emphasize the project's national importance and warn of severe repercussions for any delays. An influential industrial lobby also exerts continuous pressure for a quick resolution to facilitate their investment.

Ms. Sharma's decision will directly affect the fundamental rights and socio-cultural fabric of the tribal community, the successful implementation and timeline of a strategically vital national project, and the economic development prospects of the state. Both forcefully proceeding with acquisition or yielding to the community's demands carry significant socio-economic, political, and personal consequences for all involved stakeholders.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Ms. Ananya Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
