GS-1 SOCIETY: Communalism, Regionalism and Secularism

Q1. (10 Marks)
While often seen as distinct challenges, how might the nuanced assertion of regional identity inadvertently create fertile ground for communal polarization, despite not being directly communal in its genesis? Discuss with suitable examples.

Q2. (15 Marks)
Critically examine how the rise of competitive regionalism and identity-based communalism poses a persistent threat to India's pluralistic fabric and developmental aspirations. In this context, evaluate the effectiveness of India's constitutional secularism in fostering national unity.

GS-4 ETHICS: Civil Services Values: Integrity, Impartiality, Empathy

Q3. (10 Marks)
Discuss how an overemphasis on empathy, without the anchoring principles of integrity and impartiality, might inadvertently lead to a subversion of justice or due process in public administration. Elaborate with an example.

Q4. (20 Marks - Case Study)
You are a District Magistrate. During a routine audit of a flagship development project, you uncover significant financial irregularities and potential corruption involving the Chief Engineer, a close mentor from your probation days who has also been instrumental in the project's success. The project is critical for regional development and has garnered significant public support, but exposing the irregularities now could delay or jeopardize its completion and damage your mentor's reputation. What should the officer do? Discuss the ethical dimensions involved.
