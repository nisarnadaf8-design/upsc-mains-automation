Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY
Rohan Sharma is the District Project Coordinator (DPC) for the Rural Development Department in Mandla district, Madhya Pradesh. He is responsible for the ground-level implementation and reporting of the "Van Mitra Kalyan Yojana," a flagship state welfare scheme designed to provide direct benefit transfers to tribal communities for sustainable forest produce collection. The scheme aims to uplift thousands of vulnerable families.

As a crucial review of the scheme by the central government approaches, Rohan's immediate superior, the District Collector, instructs him to "adjust" certain beneficiary figures and disbursement amounts in the final report. The Collector explicitly states that achieving 100% target figures, irrespective of the ground reality, is essential to avoid negative political repercussions. Simultaneously, a powerful local political leader informally hints at significant career progression opportunities for Rohan if he ensures the report presents a flawless picture of the scheme's success. Rohan is aware that such manipulation would lead to genuine beneficiaries being overlooked and a misrepresentation of public funds utilisation.

The integrity of the "Van Mitra Kalyan Yojana" and the welfare of thousands of tribal families are at stake. Rohan's decision will have significant consequences for his career, the credibility of the administration, and the trust placed in government welfare programs. Both compliance and resistance carry substantial personal and professional risks.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Rohan Sharma? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
