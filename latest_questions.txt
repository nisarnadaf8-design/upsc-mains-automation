Q6. (20 Marks | 300 words) — CASE STUDY

Rajesh Kumar, a diligent and upright District Collector in Shanti Nagar, has consistently upheld procedural integrity in all administrative decisions. His office is currently processing bids for a major public infrastructure project – the expansion of the regional bus terminus – crucial for improving connectivity and reducing congestion in the rapidly urbanizing district.

A powerful state cabinet minister, Hon'ble Smt. Meena Devi, who wields significant influence, has personally called Rajesh, insisting that he ensure the tender for the bus terminus project is awarded to a specific construction company, 'Apex Buildtech'. She implicitly suggests that non-compliance could negatively impact Rajesh’s future career prospects and cooperation from the state government for other district initiatives. Apex Buildtech's bid, while technically qualified, is not the most competitive and has a history of minor compliance issues in previous projects, though none were officially substantiated. The local MLA, a close associate of the minister, also reiterates this 'request' during an official meeting, adding to the pressure.

Rajesh's decision will directly affect the financial health of the district exchequer, the quality and timely completion of a vital public asset, the morale of his subordinates, and his own professional integrity. Awarding the tender unfairly could lead to public mistrust, potential legal challenges, and sub-standard infrastructure, while refusing might invite political retribution and hinder other essential development projects in the district.

(a) Identify and discuss the ethical issues involved in this case. (50 words)
(b) What are the options available to Rajesh Kumar? Evaluate each option with its merits and demerits. (100 words)
(c) What course of action would you recommend and why? How would you handle the pressures involved? (150 words)
